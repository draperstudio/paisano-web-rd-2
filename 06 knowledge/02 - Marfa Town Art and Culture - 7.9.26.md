## 1. TOWN HISTORY AND FACTS

- Marfa was established in 1883 as a water stop and freight headquarters for the Galveston, Harrisburg and San Antonio Railway (https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/marfa-tx).
- Name origin: reportedly suggested by the wife of a railroad executive, after the character Marfa in a novel she was reading. Two attributions circulate: Marfa Strogoff in Jules Verne's *Michael Strogoff* (1876), and a character in Dostoevsky's *The Brothers Karamazov*; the attribution remains contested (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marfa,_Texas; https://bigbendsentinel.com/2023/01/18/how-marfa-got-its-name-a-theory/).
- In 1885 Marfa replaced Fort Davis as the seat of Presidio County; public records were moved that July (https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/marfa-tx).
- Population history: 900 (1900); 3,553 (1920); 3,909 (1930); a wartime peak of about 5,000 (1945); 1,788 (2020 U.S. Census) (https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/marfa-tx; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marfa,_Texas).
- Elevation: given as 4,698 feet and, in another source, 4,830 feet (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marfa,_Texas; https://www.desertusa.com/cities/tx/marfa.html).
- Marfa sits on a high-desert plateau in the Trans-Pecos, at the junction of US-90 and US-67 (https://www.texasalmanac.com/places/marfa).

## 2. THE MARFA LIGHTS

- The Marfa Lights are a reported light phenomenon visible on clear nights between Marfa and Paisano Pass in northeastern Presidio County, facing the Chinati Mountains; observers describe lights that appear colored, move, split, merge, disappear, and reappear (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marfa_lights).
- The Texas Department of Transportation built a roadside parking area 9 miles east of Marfa on US-90 for viewing; in 2003 the town used $720,000 in federal and TxDOT funds to expand it into the Marfa Mystery Lights Viewing Center, an adobe structure with restrooms, mounted binoculars, and plaques, open 24 hours and free (https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/marfa-lights).
- Reported-sighting history: a young cowhand, Robert Reed Ellison, is recorded seeing a flickering light while driving cattle through Paisano Pass in 1883 and wondering if it was an Apache campfire; early settlers Joe and Sally Humphreys reported a first sighting in 1885; the first published account appeared in the July 1957 issue of *Coronet* magazine (https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/marfa-lights).

## 3. DONALD JUDD AND THE JUDD FOUNDATION

- Donald Judd first visited Marfa in 1971 and moved from New York to Marfa as a full-time resident in 1977 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinati_Foundation).
- The Judd Foundation maintains and preserves Judd's permanently installed living and working spaces, libraries, and archives in Marfa and New York (https://juddfoundation.org/visit/marfa/).
- **La Mansana de Chinati / The Block:** Judd's Marfa residence and studio; guided visits (about 1 hour 45 minutes) reach his three main studios (installed with his early work) and his personal library of more than 13,000 books (https://juddfoundation.org/visit/marfa/).
- **The Studios:** visits to a selection of Judd's downtown spaces, including the Architecture Office, Ranch Office, Art Studio, the Cobb House, and the Whyte Building (https://juddfoundation.org/visit/marfa/).
- Pricing: $30 per person; $17 students and seniors; free for full-time residents of Presidio, Brewster, and Jeff Davis counties. Advance reservations required; visitors accompanied by a guide at all times (https://juddfoundation.org/visit/marfa/). [hours volatile, verify]
- The Judd Foundation is closed in Marfa and New York the first two weeks of August, and on major holidays (https://juddfoundation.org/visit/marfa/).
- Address listed as 104 S Highland Ave, Marfa (https://www.yelp.com/biz/judd-foundation-marfa-2).

## 4. THE CHINATI FOUNDATION

- The Chinati Foundation is a contemporary art museum created by Donald Judd; it opened to the public in 1986 as an independent nonprofit, on 340 acres at the site of former Fort D.A. Russell in Marfa (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinati_Foundation).
- Chinati was originally conceived to exhibit the work of Donald Judd, John Chamberlain, and Dan Flavin, and to preserve permanent large-scale installations by a select group of artists (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinati_Foundation).
- At the center of the collection are 100 untitled works in mill aluminum by Judd, installed in two former artillery sheds; fabricated by the Lippincott Company of Connecticut and installed over a four-year period from 1982 through 1986 (https://chinati.org/collection/donald-judd/).
- Construction and installation at the site began in 1979 with early assistance from the Dia Art Foundation (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinati_Foundation).

## 5. BALLROOM MARFA

- Ballroom Marfa is a non-collecting contemporary art space founded in 2003 by Virginia Lebermann and Fairfax Dorn, housed in a 1920s-era ballroom (https://www.ballroommarfa.org/about/).
- It commissions new work and produces exhibitions, public artworks, performances, screenings, concerts, symposia, education programs, and publications (https://www.ballroommarfa.org/about/).

## 6. PRADA MARFA

- Prada Marfa is a permanent sculptural installation by artists Elmgreen & Dragset, in the form of a freestanding Prada storefront, along US-90 in Jeff Davis County: 1.4 miles northwest of Valentine and about 26 miles northwest of Marfa (other sources give up to ~37 miles) (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prada_Marfa).
- Inaugurated October 1, 2005; construction cost $120,000; built of adobe brick, plaster, paint, glass, aluminum frame, MDF, and carpet, with architect assistance from Ronald Rael and Virginia San Fratello; displays shoes and handbags from Prada's fall/winter 2005 collection selected by Miuccia Prada (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prada_Marfa).

## 8. CULTURAL CALENDAR

- **Chinati Weekend (Open House):** annual, in October; a free public event with exhibition openings, talks, music, and meals. 2025 dates were October 10–12 (some listings cite October 9–12). The tradition began in October 1987 when Donald Judd invited friends and collaborators to Marfa for a weekend of open viewing; then called "Open House" (https://chinati.org/october-10-12-2025-chinati-weekend-2025/; https://glasstire.com/2025/10/07/chinati-weekend-offers-free-events-throughout-marfa-october-9-12/).
- **Marfa Myths:** annual music and multidisciplinary festival founded in 2014 by Ballroom Marfa and the Brooklyn music label Mexican Summer; intimate scale (capped around 800), with shows in local bars and venues (https://www.ballroommarfa.org/program/marfa-myths-2019/; https://www.papermag.com/marfa-myths-2019-1-2637883931.html).
- **Agave Festival Marfa:** multi-day festival celebrating agave through food, film, music, science, and spirits; 2026 dates June 4–7 (https://bigbendtimes.com/2026/04/01/agave-festival-marfa-returns-june-4-7-with-multi-day-cultural-programming/).
- **Viva Big Bend:** regional music festival; 2026 dates July 22–26 (https://vivabigbend.com/).
- **Trans-Pecos Festival of Music + Love:** held at El Cosmico; on hiatus until El Cosmico's new site opens (https://bigbendsentinel.com/2025/05/21/el-cosmico-announces-july-1-closure-of-current-site/).
- **Marfa Film Festival:** referenced historically; current dates NOT FOUND / needs verification.
- **Marfa Public Radio (KRTS 93.5 FM):** public radio station serving the region (https://www.marfapublicradio.org/).

## 13. SEASONAL RHYTHM

- Most Marfa restaurants and attractions are open Thursday through Sunday and closed earlier in the week (https://championtraveler.com/dates/best-time-to-visit-marfa-tx-us/).
- Many businesses run limited winter hours from roughly November through February (https://championtraveler.com/dates/best-time-to-visit-marfa-tx-us/).
- Summer temperatures regularly exceed 100°F; recorded high about 100.4°F in June/July; recorded low about 15.8°F in January (https://championtraveler.com/dates/best-time-to-visit-marfa-tx-us/).
- Spring (March–May) and fall (September–October) are commonly recommended as the most temperate times to visit (https://championtraveler.com/dates/best-time-to-visit-marfa-tx-us/).
- **Conflict to flag:** one travel aggregator states June is the busiest tourism month and fall the slowest (https://www.triphobo.com/places/marfa-united-states/best-time-to-visit). This disagrees with the Paisano's own ADR/occupancy data (see Doc 6), which shows the October Chinati Weekend as peak and January/May as lowest.
