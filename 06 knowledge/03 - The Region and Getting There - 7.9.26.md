# The Region and Getting There

Drive times and mileages are approximate and drawn from the sources named. Where sources disagree, all values are listed.

Covers how travelers reach Marfa, Texas, and the surrounding Trans-Pecos and Big Bend region: airports, drive routes, stopover towns, Big Bend National Park, the Fort Davis area, and road logistics.

---

## 1. MARFA'S LOCATION AND ROADS

- Marfa is the county seat of Presidio County, in the high desert of the Trans-Pecos region of far West Texas, between the Davis Mountains and Big Bend National Park (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marfa,_Texas).
- Marfa sits at the junction of U.S. Highways 90 and 67 (https://www.texasalmanac.com/places/marfa).
- Highways through town: US-90 (runs east–west, along San Antonio St.); US-67 (runs south, along S. Highland Ave.); Texas State Highway 17 (runs north) (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marfa,_Texas).
- Elevation: 4,698 feet (a separate source gives 4,830 feet for the town) (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marfa,_Texas).
- About 60 miles from the U.S.–Mexico border; about 20 miles south of Fort Davis on TX-17; about 18 miles west of Alpine on US-90 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marfa,_Texas).

## 2. AIRPORTS AND DRIVE TIMES TO MARFA

**Nearest commercial airports**
- El Paso International (ELP) and Midland International Air & Space Port (MAF) are the two closest, both roughly three hours by car (https://www.cityofmarfa.com/visit/page/getting-marfa).
- El Paso (ELP) → Marfa: 192 miles, about 3h 13m; route is I-10 East to Van Horn (~120 mi), then US-90 East through Valentine to Marfa (~74 mi) (https://www.travelmath.com/driving-time/from/ELP/to/Marfa,+TX; https://visitbigbend.com/drive-el-paso/). The Judd Foundation travel sheet describes the El Paso drive as about 3 hours (https://juddfoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/Marfa_Travel.pdf).
- Midland (MAF) → Marfa: 182 miles, about 2h 54m (a second source: 184 miles, ~3h 08m) (https://www.travelmath.com/drive-distance/from/MAF/to/Marfa,+TX; https://www.rome2rio.com/s/Midland-Odessa-Airport-MAF/Marfa).

**Marfa Municipal Airport (general aviation)**
- IATA MRF; public-use county airport 3 miles north of Marfa; 816 acres at 4,849 feet elevation; no scheduled airline service (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marfa_Municipal_Airport).
- Alpine–Casparis Municipal Airport is another general-aviation option, about 26 miles / ~30 minutes from Marfa (https://www.cityofmarfa.com/visit/page/getting-marfa).

**Rail**
- Amtrak's Sunset Limited passes through Marfa but does not stop; the nearest station is Alpine, 26 miles (42 km) northeast (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marfa,_Texas).

**Drive distances/times from major Texas cities** (from road-trip aggregators; approximate: see caveats)
- Austin → Marfa: ~429 miles, ~6h 23m fastest (alternate routes ~480 mi / ~8h) (https://lazytrips.com/trip/road-trip-from-austin-to-marfa-texas).
- Dallas → Marfa: ~670 miles, ~10h 40m (https://lazytrips.com/trip/road-trip-from-dallas-to-marfa).
- Houston → Marfa: ~590 miles, ~9h 20m (https://lazytrips.com/trip/road-trip-from-houston-to-marfa).
- San Antonio → Marfa: ~407 miles, ~6h 26m (https://www.rome2rio.com/s/San-Antonio/Marfa).
- El Paso → Marfa: 192 miles, ~3h 13m (see above).

## 3. STOPOVER TOWNS AND NEARBY DESTINATIONS (distance/time from Marfa)

- **Alpine**: Brewster County seat, Sul Ross State University, Amtrak station; 26 miles / ~27 min via US-90 East (https://www.travelmath.com/drive-distance/from/Marfa,+TX/to/Alpine,+TX).
- **Fort Davis**: gateway to the Davis Mountains and Fort Davis National Historic Site; ~21 miles / ~27 min via TX-17 North (https://www.distance-cities.com/distance-marfa-tx-to-fort-davis-tx).
- **Marathon**: small town, northern gateway toward Big Bend via US-385; 57 miles / ~1h 4m via US-90 East (https://drivedistance.com/from-marfa-tx-to-marathon-tx).
- **Fort Stockton**: town at I-10; 92 miles / ~1h 45m via US-67 North (https://www.trippy.com/distance/Marfa-to-Fort-Stockton).
- **Valentine (Prada Marfa)**: the Prada Marfa installation is at 14880 US-90, Valentine, on the south side of US-90 less than 2 miles from Valentine; about 37 miles northwest of Marfa (sources range 26–37 miles) (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prada_Marfa; https://www.ballroommarfa.org/prada-marfa/map/).
- **Balmorhea**: home of Balmorhea State Park and the San Solomon Springs pool; ~58 miles / ~1h 8m via TX-17 through Fort Davis (Atlas Obscura says "about 50 miles north") (https://www.trippy.com/distance/Marfa-to-Balmorhea; https://www.atlasobscura.com/articles/balmorhea-state-park-pool-history).
  - Balmorhea pool: world's largest spring-fed swimming pool; up to 25 feet deep; covers 1.3 acres; holds 3.5 million gallons; more than 15 million gallons flow through per day (https://tpwmagazine.com/parks/balmorhea-state-parks-spring-fed-pool-is-a-west-texas-oasis/).
- **Terlingua / Study Butte**: service settlement at Big Bend's western approach; 110 miles / ~2h 33m via TX-118 South (https://www.trippy.com/distance/Marfa-to-Terlingua).
- **Sanderson**: town on US-90 (Terrell County seat); 111 miles / ~2h 6m via US-90 East (https://www.distance-cities.com/distance-sanderson-tx-to-marfa-tx).
- **Marfa Lights Viewing Area**: official viewing center 9 miles east of Marfa on US-90 toward Alpine; built 2003 (https://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/texas/marfa/attractions/marfa-mystery-lights-viewing-area/a/poi-sig/1353314/1315496).

## 4. BIG BEND NATIONAL PARK

- From Marfa the route runs TX-118 South (via Alpine and Study Butte/Terlingua) to the Maverick Junction (west) entrance near Study Butte; Marfa to the park is about 2h 29m by car (https://visitbigbend.com/drive-el-paso/; https://www.rome2rio.com/s/Marfa/Big-Bend-National-Park).
- From Alpine: TX-118 south ~78 miles to Study Butte, then ~3 miles to the west entrance, then ~26 miles east to Panther Junction headquarters (https://visitbigbend.com/drive-el-paso/; https://www.nps.gov/bibe/planyourvisit/directions.htm).
- From Marathon (northern approach): US-385 south ~40 miles to Persimmon Gap; NPS says Marathon is ~70 miles from park headquarters (https://www.nps.gov/bibe/planyourvisit/directions.htm).
- NPS advisory: distances between towns and services are considerable, and GPS/app mapping "do not work well" in the remote area; paper maps advised (https://www.nps.gov/bibe/planyourvisit/directions.htm).

**Fees and logistics**
- Entrance pass $30 per private vehicle (valid 7 days); $25 motorcycle; $15 per person on foot/bicycle; $55 Big Bend annual pass; $80 America the Beautiful pass (https://www.nps.gov/bibe/planyourvisit/fees.htm).
- Fees are paid by credit/debit card only; no cash (https://www.shakaguide.com/article/big-bend/big-bend-national-park-know-before-you-go).
- Busy season is generally November through April, peaking on 3-day holiday weekends, Thanksgiving week, the Christmas holidays, and mid-January through mid-April; advance reservations essential then (https://www.nps.gov/bibe/planyourvisit/basicinfo.htm).

**Elevations and key sights**
- Park elevations range from under 1,800 feet along the Rio Grande to nearly 8,000 feet in the Chisos Mountains; the Chisos Basin sits at 5,400 feet and has the park's only full-service restaurant and the Chisos Mountains Lodge (https://www.nps.gov/bibe/planyourvisit/basicinfo.htm).
- The Window: V-shaped pour-off that drains the Chisos Basin during rainstorms (https://wheninyourstate.com/texas/big-bend-national-park-texas/).
- Santa Elena Canyon: limestone walls over 1,500 feet above the Rio Grande; canyon runs ~8 miles (https://visitbigbend.com/santa-elena/).
- Hot Springs: natural ~105°F springs on the Rio Grande, ~0.25-mile flat trail from the parking area (https://wheninyourstate.com/texas/big-bend-national-park-texas/).
- Boquillas Canyon: the park's longest and deepest canyon (https://jeffminich.com/2019/09/29/big-bend-national-park/).
- Boquillas Crossing: port of entry across the Rio Grande to Boquillas del Carmen, Mexico; carries a crossing fee plus optional village-transport fee, not included in park entry; passport required (verify hours/documents at NPS) (https://www.hipcamp.com/journal/camping/big-bend-camping-costs).

**Big Bend Ranch State Park (separate)**
- Largest state park in Texas, more than 300,000 acres, west of the national park between Lajitas and Presidio (https://tpwd.texas.gov/state-parks/big-bend-ranch).
- River Road / El Camino del Rio (FM 170 / TX-170): scenic drive along the Rio Grande between Lajitas and Presidio; commonly cited as ~50 miles; the "Big Hill" has one of the steepest highway grades in Texas (https://visitbigbend.com/camino-del-rio/; https://backroadplanet.com/el-camino-del-rio-big-bend-ranch-state-park/).

## 5. FORT DAVIS AREA

**McDonald Observatory**
- Operated by The University of Texas at Austin; on Mt. Locke in the Davis Mountains, about 16 miles northwest of Fort Davis; elevation over 6,700 feet (Otto Struve Telescope at 6,790 ft) (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McDonald_Observatory).
- Frank N. Bash Visitors Center: 3640 Dark Sky Dr., Fort Davis, TX 79734 (https://mcdonaldobservatory.org/visitors/directions).
- About 37 miles / ~51 min from Marfa (https://www.distance-cities.com/distance-marfa-tx-to-mcdonald-observatory-tx).
- Star Parties: schedule varies by season, 2 hours long, ticket range $5–$25; open-air constellation tour plus telescope viewing; check-in at the Bash Visitors Center; 432-426-3640 (https://mcdonaldobservatory.org/visitors/programs/evening-programs).
- Special Viewing Nights: 36-inch telescope night, 2.5 hours, $100 ($80 members); 82-inch telescope night, 2.5 hours, $150 ($120 members), select nights (https://mcdonaldobservatory.org/visitors/programs/evening-programs).

**Davis Mountains State Park**
- 2,709 acres in the Davis Mountains, Jeff Davis County; a "sky island" between roughly 5,000 and 6,000 feet; connected to Fort Davis National Historic Site by ~4 miles of trails (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Davis_Mountains_State_Park; https://tpwd.texas.gov/state-parks/davis-mountains).

**Indian Lodge**
- Historic 39-room adobe-style lodge inside Davis Mountains State Park; has a restaurant and a swimming pool (guests only); reopened May 2025 after a $3.5 million renovation (https://tpwd.texas.gov/state-parks/indian-lodge; https://www.texasmonthly.com/travel/finding-respite-davis-mountains-state-park-historic-indian-lodge/).

**Fort Davis National Historic Site**
- Preserves the remains of a late-1800s frontier U.S. Army cavalry post near the town of Fort Davis (https://www.nps.gov/foda/planyourvisit/nearbyattractions.htm).

**The Scenic Loop (TX-118 / TX-166)**
- A roughly 74–75-mile loop drive through the Davis Mountains, beginning and ending in Fort Davis (https://tpwd.texas.gov/state-parks/davis-mountains).

## 6. THE DRIVE AND PRACTICAL LOGISTICS

- Between towns in West Texas there can be stretches of 70 to 120 miles with no gas stations; a sign east of Sanderson on US-90 reads "no services next 121 miles" (to Del Rio) (https://texashighways.com/travel/what-i-learned-driving-an-ev-to-far-west-texas/).
- Inside Big Bend there are normally two fuel locations, at Panther Junction and Rio Grande Village. As of July 29, 2025, both were reported unavailable "until further notice" after July 4 flooding; travelers were advised to fuel up in Marathon and Terlingua before entering. This is time-sensitive and must be re-verified (https://www.marfapublicradio.org/news/2025-07-29/gas-and-diesel-unavailable-at-big-bend-national-park-until-further-notice).
- Recommended fuel-stop towns leaving I-10: Alpine, Marathon, Terlingua/Study Butte, Panther Junction (https://solsalute.com/blog/big-bend-national-park/).
- Cell service is very spotty across the Big Bend area; fairly reliable only in the Chisos Basin (Verizon) and near Panther Junction (https://www.nps.gov/bibe/planyourvisit/basicinfo.htm).
- Permanent Border Patrol interior checkpoints in the region include US-67 North about 4.5 miles south of Marfa; I-10 East near Sierra Blanca; and US-90 East near Brackettville/Uvalde (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Border_Patrol_interior_checkpoints).
