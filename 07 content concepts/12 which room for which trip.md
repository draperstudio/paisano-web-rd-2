# 12 Which Room For Which Trip

**Goal** — Convert the decided-but-unsure. The strategy's conversion section names this exactly: "which room to pick for the trip they described." Distinct from 03 (the stay, told straight): that one earns trust in the product, this one closes the pick.

**Move** — Ready-to-book people from hovering over the room list to committing to one. Removing the last micro-decision is the move.

**Person & want** — All segments at the end. Practical plane: two nights, a dog, a west-facing window, whatever their trip actually is. Live want: competence (pick right, no regret).

**Message** — The room types, and which trip each one fits: the one-night road stop gets a Queen Historic, the long Chinati weekend gets a Junior Suite or 1BR, the evening-outside people get a Mezzanine Patio (private walled patio, outdoor fireplace) or a Courtyard Patio king, the four-sleeper trip gets a 2 Queen Historic, and the occasion gets the Rock Hudson Suite. The delineating features that create real jumps in value, per the SNT call, are exactly the matcher's raw material: patio, balcony, courtyard side vs street side, and the sitting area.

**Angle** — Guide, in catalog form. Trip-first, not room-first: start from the trip they're taking, land on the room.

**Execution** — A compact matcher near the rooms section or on the booking path: six types, each tagged to the trips it serves, rate honesty attached. Feeds the PMS handoff with the decision already made. Tag by trip facts (one night, two people, a dog, a group), never persona names: "The Artist's Retreat" is exactly the boutique-hotel cosplay this site doesn't do.

**Draws on** — Knowledge: 06 knowledge/07 (the nine classes, callouts, delineating features, open items), 06 dossier (reconfiguration, rates). Media: render-bedroom, lobby-seating. Note: the matcher is also where the booking-engine gap gets covered, since StayNTouch can't show per-room features; the site carries them.

**Board** — Band 6 (stacked cards) is the natural carrier.
