# 09 The Drive

**Goal** — Reframe the biggest first-timer objection (it is far) as the reason to go. Marfa is three hours from the nearest airport and six and a half from Austin, and that remoteness is exactly what the person is buying.

**Move** — Awareness and early consideration: from "someday, it's so far" to "the drive is part of the trip." This is the postponement killer for first-timers the way the Tuesday-food answer is for road-trippers.

**Person & want** — First-time visitors from the Texas metros and the coasts. Experiential plane: they want the West Texas feeling, and the feeling starts on US-90, not at check-in. Live want: identity (the kind of person who drives to Marfa) and curiosity.

**Message** — El Paso three hours, Austin six and a half, and the drive is high-desert grassland, mountains, and small towns the whole way. Come through Van Horn and Valentine, or make it a circuit with Big Bend and Fort Davis.

**Angle** — Guide crossed with scene. Routes with real stops and times, carried by landscape photography and restraint.

**Execution** — A compact routes section: the three or four ways in, drive times stated plainly, what to stop for, and the Big Bend circuit for the longer trip. Feeds the West Texas travel adjacency on social. One trap named up front: every Marfa piece ever written opens with the drive and the light and the nothingness, so this is a route sheet, not an ode. The photography carries the feeling; the words stay logistics.

**Draws on** — Knowledge: 03 region doc (airports, routes, times, stopover towns), 04 doc (the desert itself: mile-high grassland, pronghorn). Media: marfa-street-sunset; needs landscape/road media eventually, which is a media request to note.

**Board** — No band carries this yet. It surfaced from strategy and knowledge, not the board, and needs its own exploration.
