# 01 Arrival

**Goal** — Make the person want to be at the Paisano before a single fact lands. Serves the big goal by doing what the strategy says the place does: persuading before the copy does.

**Move** — Someone who knows Marfa exists but feels nothing about this hotel, to feeling something. Also re-lands the person who saved a neon photo on Instagram months ago: this is that place.

**Person & want** — The art-trip couple and the saver of places. Experiential plane: they want the trip to feel like something. Live want: identity (I am the kind of person who goes to Marfa) and curiosity.

**Message** — This place feels like nowhere else you can stay. Said with almost no words.

**Angle** — Scene. The courtyard at dusk, the neon against the sky, the building at night. Little or no text.

**Execution** — The site's opening: full-bleed photography, the wordmark, and nearly nothing else. The one theatrical brand gesture (the preloader script) lives here; the page that follows never repeats it at that scale.

**Draws on** — Media: neon-sign-day, neon-sign-closeup, courtyard-fountain, facade-entrance, marfa-street-sunset. Knowledge: none needed; this section carries no facts.

**Board** — Band 1 / band 10 (oversized script, preloader) and band 3 (hero derivations) are the expressions already exploring this.
