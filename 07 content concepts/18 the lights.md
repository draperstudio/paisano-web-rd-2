# 18 The Lights

**Goal** — Claim the one story in Marfa no competitor can touch: the hotel and the Marfa Lights share a name. The first recorded sighting was at Paisano Pass in 1883, a cowhand named Robert Reed Ellison wondering if he was seeing Apache campfires. The hotel is named for the same pass.

**Move** — Curiosity-stage people from "Marfa has mystery lights" (which everyone knows) to a fact only this hotel can tell them. The connection converts a town-level attraction into a brand-level story.

**Person & want** — Broad: every first-timer asks about the lights. Live want: curiosity, and the retellable detail (status in miniature: the thing you say at dinner).

**Message** — The first recorded sighting was at Paisano Pass in 1883. The hotel is named for the same pass. Nine miles east, the viewing center is open all night; go after dinner.

**Angle** — Archive crossed with guide: the 1883 story told straight, then the practical (where, when, free, 24 hours). No mystery-mongering: the lights are tourist territory and the art crowd knows it, so this is a documented account and directions, never "some say."

**Execution** — A short page or a distinct block inside the Marfa guide, whichever the outline step decides; the concept is the pairing of story and directions. Also a natural evening companion piece to the bar.

**Draws on** — Knowledge: 02 Marfa doc section 2 (Ellison 1883, viewing center, distances). Media: needs a night-sky or viewing-center image eventually; media request to note.

**Board** — No band; surfaced from knowledge. Candidate for the micro-copy expression (a single strange fact, placed quietly).
