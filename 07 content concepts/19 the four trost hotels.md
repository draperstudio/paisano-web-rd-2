# 19 The Four Trost Hotels

**Goal** — Turn provenance into a trip. The Paisano, the El Capitan in Van Horn, the Gage in Marathon, and the Paso del Norte in El Paso are documented as Trost & Trost's four famous West Texas hotels, and people actually drive that circuit. The Paisano gets to narrate it because it is one of the four.

**Move** — West Texas travelers and architecture people from a one-town trip to a regional itinerary with the Paisano as a fixed stop. Deepens the drive concept (09) into a reason the drive exists.

**Person & want** — Road-trippers, design and history people, repeat Texans looking for a new frame on familiar ground. Live want: identity and competence (the traveler who knows the good version of the trip).

**Message** — One architect built the four great hotels of West Texas between 1912 and 1930. You can sleep in all four, and the circuit between them is a trip people actually drive.

**Angle** — Archive crossed with guide: Trost's story once (Toledo, Chicago and Sullivan's influence, El Paso, reinforced concrete), then the circuit with real distances.

**Execution** — A standalone piece linked from the landmark and drive sections. Generous to the other three hotels by design; the confidence of naming your peers is the brand register working.

**Draws on** — Knowledge: 01 building doc section 1 (Trost, the four hotels, dates), 03 region doc (distances, routes). Media: archival-henry-trost; needs the other hotels eventually, media request to note.

**Board** — No band; surfaced from knowledge.
