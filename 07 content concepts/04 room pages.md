# 04 Room Pages

**Goal** — One detail page per room type, the Bowery model: every type gets its own page with real photography, a features list, an amenities list, and a direct book path. This is where the "mirror database" from the SNT call becomes web: StayNTouch shows descriptions at type level only and cannot carry per-room detail, so these pages are the only place the full room story exists. Top-tier concept: room and bathroom detail is what the strategy says decides trust at $425.

**Move** — Comparison-stage planners from evaluating to booking a specific room type. The homepage rooms section (03) earns trust in the product overall; these pages close a specific type. The matcher (12) routes people here.

**Person & want** — All segments at the comparison stage, deepest for the art-trip couple weighing the Paisano against the St. George and what $425 buys elsewhere. Practical and Fear & Risk planes: exactly what am I getting. Live want: security, then competence (picked right).

**Message** — Per type, everything true about it in one place: bed and bath configuration, square footage, what outdoor space it has (walled patio with fireplace, courtyard patio, sun porch, rooftop terrace), which side it faces, occupancy, rate, and the named room inside it where one exists (the James Dean Room lives in Queen Historic; the Rock Hudson Suite is its own page).

**Angle** — Catalog crossed with demonstration. The internal definitions in 06 knowledge/07 are already written in the right register ("The most basic room type. One queen bed, a European bath with shower only, north facing courthouse views.") — the pages keep that plainness and let photography carry the persuasion.

**Execution** — A page per class with type-to-type navigation across the set (the Bowery reference's tab row), features and amenities as designed lists rather than paragraphs, photography per type, and Book Now passing the type into the PMS. A couple of different page treatments get explored in round two off band 4's three references (Bowery structure, Frati editorial spread, Maison Charlotte photo-and-field). Honesty guards from 06 knowledge/07: renders labeled until the January 2027 product exists, no Juliet balconies (renovation removes them), no 2BR promised forward (class dies), and current classes only — the six-type set is coming, not here.

**Draws on** — Knowledge: 06 knowledge/07 (the nine classes, per-class definitions, features, callouts, open items), 06 dossier (rates, phasing). Media: render-bedroom, render-bathroom, lobby-seating; per-type room photography is the biggest media request this concept generates.

**Board** — Band 4 is this concept's band: the Bowery room detail page (type nav, features/amenities lists), the Frati Home spread, and the Maison Charlotte photo-left field-right treatment. Bands 5/6 (carousel, stacked cards) feed the in-page galleries.
