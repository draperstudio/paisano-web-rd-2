# 02 Open Now, Being Restored

**Goal** — State plainly what this is and defuse the one doubt that kills bookings: is this place actually renovated or coasting on the old building? The honest answer, stated with confidence, is the orientation.

**Move** — Someone who knows the Paisano (maybe stayed at the old one, maybe heard it was tired) from knowing-but-wary to feeling something. One stage, honestly: full trust is built later by 03 and 07; this passage's job is to open the door without blowing it.

**Person & want** — Repeat Marfa visitors comparing against their last stay, and first-timers reading between the lines. Fear & Risk plane: paying $425-level money for a dated room. Live want: security, not being wrong.

**Message** — The Paisano is back. New ownership that lives in Marfa, open every day of the restoration, being restored to what it should be.

**Angle** — Expository with a personal edge. Said straight, once, in one passage. Candor is the mechanism, with the trap named: state the facts, never narrate the honesty itself. "We're not overpromising" is overpromising. The reader gets the candor from the facts being plainly stated, not from being told the brand is honest.

**Execution** — One short passage high on the page, right after arrival. The owners-live-here and 24-hour-desk operating facts belong to this concept, not buried in a footer.

**Draws on** — Knowledge: 06 dossier (ownership, July 1 close, phasing), 01 building doc. Strategy: emphasis 02, primary message.

**Board** — The micro-copy expression may carry the operating facts as small text moments rather than a paragraph.
