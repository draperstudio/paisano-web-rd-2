# 16 Giant

**Goal** — Contain the Hollywood story by giving it one great home. The strategy is explicit that Giant is not the lead; the way to keep it from leaking into everything is a single page that tells it completely, catches the search traffic ("hotel from Giant"), and sets the frame: the building existed 26 years before the film and has run 70 years since.

**Move** — Movie-searchers who have never heard of the new Paisano from arriving via the film to caring about the hotel. They come for the film; the page hands them the building.

**Person & want** — A distinct entry segment: film people, Texas-history people, nostalgia trips. Live want: curiosity, identity (pilgrimage travel).

**Message** — In 1955 the cast of Giant lived here for a summer. Here is that story, told from primary material, and here is the building it happened in, which is the bigger story. The connection is physical and bookable, not nostalgic: four rooms carry cast names today, the James Dean Room (223, a Queen Historic), the Elizabeth Taylor Suite (212), the Dennis Hopper Suite (220), and the Rock Hudson Suite (211, the signature suite). That the most famous name sits on the most basic room class is the kind of fact this page states and lets sit.

**Angle** — Archive. Photographs, dates, and documented facts; no Hollywood cosplay, no memorabilia-museum tone.

**Execution** — One standalone page, linked once from the landmark section. It absorbs all Giant material so the rest of the site can hold the one-line discipline.

**Draws on** — Knowledge: 01 building doc section 10 (the film's role, documented), 06 knowledge/07 (the named rooms, their classes and numbers). Media: archival-facade-1940s, archival photos. One flag from 07: the Dennis Hopper Suite's room class dies in the renovation and the name's fate is undecided, so the page should not hard-promise all four rooms forward.

**Board** — Band 18's archival treatment extends here.
