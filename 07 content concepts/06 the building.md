# 06 The Building

**Goal** — Convert provenance into feeling. No competitor in Marfa has anything comparable to the building, and the strategy says history is working material spent in steady doses, not emptied at once.

**Move** — Someone who feels a pull toward the place to feeling it is the real thing. Values and identity planes: these people choose the authentic original over the newer copy, and this section hands them the reasons.

**Person & want** — Art-trip couples especially; people who go to Marfa for Judd choose buildings the same way. Live want: identity (I stay at the real thing) and status quietly underneath (the story they retell at dinner).

**Message** — A 1930 Henry Trost landmark, one of the four famous Trost hotels of West Texas, at the center of town for 95 years. One line for Giant: in 1955 the cast lived here for a summer. The building existed 26 years before the film and has run 70 years since.

**Angle** — Archive. Primary material: the 1940s photograph, the National Register listing, the original tile, the fountain, the neon. Captions carry single facts.

**Execution** — The landmark section: archival photography beside current photography of the same details, the Trost story told once and well, Giant deliberately downplayed to one confident line. Deeper Trost and restoration-craft material spends later as its own pieces.

**Draws on** — Knowledge: 01 building doc (Trost, NRHP 1978, construction), 04 doc restoration-craft section. Media: archival-facade-1940s, archival-henry-trost, archival-facade-entrance, facade-crest-detail, neon-sign-closeup.

**Board** — Band 18 (facade photo section with the 1940s photograph) is this concept's expression already forming.
