# 20 What Gets Saved

**Goal** — Tell the restoration as craft, not construction updates. The strategy lists historic restoration as an adjacency: what gets saved, what gets rebuilt, and the trades and decisions involved in restoring a 1930 building. Distinct from 07 (the timeline): that is status and dates; this is the decisions and the people doing the work.

**Move** — People who feel something for the building to trusting the owners with it. The deepest trust move on the site: showing the decisions proves the stewardship the orientation passage claims.

**Person & want** — The design-and-provenance crowd, plus locals watching what the new owners do to their landmark. Values plane: do it right. Live want: security about the building itself, curiosity about the craft.

**Message** — This is what we are keeping, this is what has to be rebuilt, and this is who is doing the work: the tile, the plaster, the neon, the fountain, decision by decision.

**Angle** — Process crossed with profile: the trades as people, the decisions as the story. Reporting planned once, feeding several depths per the strategy.

**Execution** — A journal-like section that grows during the renovation: one decision or one room per entry, dated. The web home of the room-by-room series; each entry also feeds social.

**Draws on** — Knowledge: 04 doc (restoration craft section), 01 building doc (original details), 06 dossier (phasing). Media: facade-crest-detail; needs work-in-progress photography, media request to note.

**Board** — Band 18's archival treatment and the data-table expressions both serve entries.
