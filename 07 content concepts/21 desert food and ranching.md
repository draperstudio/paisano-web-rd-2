# 21 Desert Food and Ranching

**Goal** — Give Meseta a food identity before it has a menu. The strategy lists desert food and ranching as an adjacency; the knowledge holds a real region (the largest hot desert in North America, mile-high grassland, working ranches back to Milton Faver in 1857) that no other kitchen in town is working from.

**Move** — Food-driven travelers from "Marfa has a few restaurants" to anticipating one specific kitchen. Pre-opening, the story is what there is to publish, and it only works if it is real: named ranchers, named places. Terroir talk with no restaurant open is exactly the aspirational-branding smell this must not have.

**Person & want** — The food-and-culture segment inside the art-trip crowd; they plan trips around restaurants. Live want: curiosity, status (early to the thing).

**Message** — The Chihuahuan desert is a food region: the ranches, the ingredients, and the history behind them, and a chef, Brian Espinosa, cooking from it. Targeted fall 2026, framed as coming.

**Angle** — Profile and expository: the region first, the ranchers as people, the chef's read on it. Documentary register; no menu promises before there is a menu.

**Execution** — A standalone piece linked from the food section, built to grow into Meseta's own page at opening. First profile candidates: a rancher behind the future menu.

**Draws on** — Knowledge: 04 doc (desert, ranching, foodways), 06 dossier (Meseta timeline, chef). Media: needs ranch and landscape photography; media request to note.

**Board** — No band; surfaced from strategy and knowledge.
