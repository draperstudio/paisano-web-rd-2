# 02 title over image — expressions doc

**Version:** V1 · 7.12.26 · created from the board pass (Buck's board comment, worked by AI — Buck QAs)
**Type:** composition concept — a hero-scale section pattern (title centered over an image block), with branched variants inside it.

## What it is

Centered titling over an image block, with text below — the straightforward hero-ish move Buck started ripping early. The band carries branched sub-directions: versions where the image cycles through (possibly slightly askew), versions built around the logo with text above and below it, and two later variant rows — big titling with a small offset image and microtext (points at 17 small image offset), and the wordmark large up top with an image or the illustration below.

## What Buck likes

The core is workable rather than beloved: "It's just Paisano over the image and then text below and OK whatever, it's fine." The energy is in the variants: the image cycling ("there are a few here, though, where the image is cycled through... with the images slightly askew — it could be a preloader or just the hero"), the logo-with-above-and-below-text offshoot ("there's some shit there"), and the small-offset-image row ("there's something there to it where we can do big titling and script or whatever with image offset"). The fifth-row idea he'd take: "It could be Hotel Paisano bigger up top and then an image down below or the fucking illustration."

## Control for (locked)

- **Grid (alignment):** centered — the title balances on the middle axis over the image block.
- **Zones:** title over image block, supporting text below — that stack is the pattern.
- **Hierarchy:** title leads, image supports, text below carries detail.
- **Brand:** the wordmark question runs through this band — "we have to figure out the script and stuff that's going to affect this." Standard use keeps "Hotel Paisano" together.

## Leave open (variation space)

- **Motion (unvetted control):** the image-cycling variant — images cycling through, possibly slightly askew. Could be a preloader or a hero, **but not both this and the script thing (01) as the hero + preloader pair.**
- **Imagery:** which image, crop, and containment — fills from 04 media at production.
- **Type:** faces, casing, sizing — open everywhere (see settled.md: type not landed).
- **The fifth-row variant:** wordmark large up top, image or illustration below — worth cycling as its own version.
- **Space / Balance / Color.**

## Negative

- No mixing typefaces: "there are some here that are a mix of types, like typefaces. That's kind of whack. We don't do that."
- No all-caps serif treatment of the wordmark: "I don't like the all-caps serif. We don't do that with our Hotel Paisano wordmark."
- Solo "Paisano" as a wordmark is out ("I don't like the Paisano. We never do that") — the one liked solo-Paisano in this band is the script one.
- The first variant of the third row died: "I don't necessarily like that first one on the left, which is the Hotel Paisano in the image to the right, big and kind of in line."
