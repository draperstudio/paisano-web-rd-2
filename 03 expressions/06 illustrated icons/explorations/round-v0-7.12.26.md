# Round — v0 — 7.12.26

## What was tried

Three live variations testing HOW illustration-type icons deploy as a garnish, mounted in a real room-detail section (Mezzanine Patio, real catalog facts, current photography with the honest king-room label). Routes:

- `/e06/var-1` — framed garnish row: three icons with small serif labels between hairline rules, under the room block. The closest read to the soft-cashmere ref's icon row.
- `/e06/var-2` — single stationery device: one icon above the small-caps section label, engraved-letterhead style. The minimum-count end of the axis.
- `/e06/var-3` — icons inside 16's receipt register: a small icon at the left of each dashed-rule row. Tests whether the garnish survives inside the locked table register.

CALL: the icon set itself is a generated stand-in — four etched-stationery-style icons (French doors, transom, fireplace, bath tub; subjects pulled from real Mezzanine Patio features in the catalog) made with an image model, levels-pushed to sit directly on the cream ground. The real set is an asset effort on `05 graphics/to-make.md` and should share a hand with 27 illustration style, per this doc's own open question. Deployment is the axis judged here, not the icons.

## What was learned

- Execution problem, fixed in-round: generated icons carried toned paper backgrounds that read as beige boxes on the cream ground. Regeneration on white plus a levels push (sharp, linear 1.25/-45) dissolved them. Any gathered/made icon set needs flat-white or transparent ground as a spec line.
- Axis finding: the icons want a heavier line below ~30px. At register-row size (var-3) the fine etching hand goes faint; at 40px+ (var-1) it holds. The asset effort should cut two weights or one weight tuned for ~36–48px.
- Var-2 (single device over the section label) is the quietest and most stationery-native read — closest to "add in with the little framing, small text, and stuff."
- Var-3 is legible but the icons compete with the dashed rules; the register may already be at its ornament budget. Flagged, not killed — Buck's call.
- Three icons (var-1) does not trip the "too many" negative; the St. George failure was a dense multi-row field, not a count of three.
