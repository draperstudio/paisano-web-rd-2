# 06 illustrated icons — expressions doc

**Version:** V1 · 7.12.26 · created from the board pass (Buck's board comment, worked by AI — Buck QAs)
**Type:** composition ingredient with an asset dependency — illustration-style icons used sparingly with framing and small text. The icons themselves are on 05 graphics/to-make.md.

## What it is

Small illustration-type icons worked into compositions — the idea surfaced when the soft cashmere reference from band 04 was left uncropped here, showing its icon row. Paired with "the little framing, small text, and stuff." The icons would need to be made or gathered.

## What Buck likes

"I thought maybe we could do some illustration-type icons... that's something we could add in with the little framing, small text, and stuff." The restraint matters as much as the icons.

## Control for (locked)

- **Imagery (share):** supporting at most · garnish, not feature · few in number ("I don't want to do too many")
- **Technique (graphic vocabulary):** illustration-style icons · drawn, not interface-icon geometry · hand shared with 27 illustration style
- **Accents:** icons "add in with the little framing, small text, and stuff" · a pairing suggestion, not a ban on other uses (audit fix: an earlier draft said "never floating alone" — Buck never prohibited that)

## Leave open (variation space)

- Which icons (subjects) — driven by content at production; make-or-gather is open ("either need to make or go gather").
- Where they deploy — any composition that can take a garnish row.
- Construction/edge/massing of the icon set (graphic controls) — untouched by feedback; belongs to the asset effort when it runs.

## Negative

- Too many icons — the St. George Marguerite reference "has too many."
- Too playful an illustration style — same reference: "the illustration style is too playful."

## Open questions

- Whether the icon set is its own asset effort on to-make (recorded there as "illustration-type icons" — AI, review) and how it sequences against 27 illustration style, since the two should share a hand.
