# Round — v0 — 7.12.26

## What was tried

First build of the band. Three live variations of a contained history panel ("The Building" — dedicated June 1930, Trost & Trost, the Giant summer; all facts from 06 knowledge/01) holding every locked zone: top framing line, title, main block, small bit below, text over the image, bottom framing line. Contained with border and margins per the lock; no full bleed. Routes:

- `/e08/var-1` — moderate ladder: five sizes, restrained spread, tracked-serif framing lines inside the bordered panel.
- `/e08/var-2` — dramatic spread: display-scale title (text-8xl) against 10px framing lines; the spread between sizes pushed as far as the register allows.
- `/e08/var-3` — the 12-kinship cycle this doc asks for: framing lines leave the panel and pin to the viewport edges at micro scale, testing whether 08 and 12 are one system.

One axis varied: hierarchy steepness / where the framing lines live. Faces, casing (tracked uppercase serif — allowed by lock 1), zones, and containment held constant.

## What was learned

- Execution problem, fixed in-round: the over-image line originally sat at the image bottom, colliding with the landmark plaques the facade photo carries in its lower third — and at the top it needed a quiet ink scrim over the bright sky. Flagged as a CALL in the component: over-image text on this photo wants the top edge plus a functional scrim.
- Axis finding: the dramatic spread (var-2) is where the band earns its name — at moderate spread (var-1) the composition reads as a generic centered stack and the framing lines stop feeling structural. Named comparison: the ref's power comes from its menu row and bottom caps line being wildly smaller than the title; var-2 reproduces that relationship, var-1 flattens it.
- Kinship finding: var-3 works — the framing lines at the viewport edge read as one system with 12 edge microtext, and the panel border keeps containment even with the frame text outside it. 08-at-composition-scale and 12-at-micro-scale look like two zoom levels of the same idea, not two ideas.
- The receipt-register em-dash line and the framing lines share a register; no conflict observed.
