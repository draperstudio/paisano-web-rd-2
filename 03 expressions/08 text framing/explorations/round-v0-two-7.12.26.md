# Round two — v0, 7.12.26

Nothing removed. Vars 1–3 stand as round one.

## What re-studying the ref exposed

The Oliver du Puy ref is a TWO-PANEL composition on a dark ground, and
round one flattened it into a single vertical stack. In the ref: the
left panel is a tall photo with tracked caps overlaid at mid-frame
(words spaced apart across the width); the right panel is a light
sheet framed by text — a nav row across the top, a caps row across
the base, and between them a centered small-caps title, a paragraph
with every line centered (not justified), a single dot glyph, and a
three-line colophon.

## New variation

- var-4 — the faithful split: ink ground; courtyard photo left with
  HOTEL PAISANO / MARFA AND TEXAS spaced across mid-frame; cream sheet
  right framed by MENU / HOTEL PAISANO / BACK on top and THE GRANDE
  DAME / OF WEST TEXAS at the base, with the Building paragraph
  centered line by line, the dot, and the Trost & Trost colophon.

## Held

Serif everywhere (the ref's sans is translated, not copied). Body at
13.5px within lock 6. Framing is structural top AND bottom on the
sheet. The full-bleed negative isn't violated — the composition is
contained inside the ink margin.

## Honest note

The mid-frame overlay reads weakest where the fountain is busiest;
the ref's photo has a quieter mid-zone. If this advances, the photo
swap (quieter mid-band) is the first dial to turn.
