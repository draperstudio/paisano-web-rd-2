# 08 text framing — expressions doc

**Version:** V1 · 7.12.26 · created from the board pass (Buck's board comment, worked by AI — Buck QAs)
**Type:** composition concept — a section pattern where type frames the composition top and bottom, with a wide ladder of text sizes. **No direct explorations yet.**

## What it is

From the high-contrast serif typographic framing reference: text framing the composition at top and bottom, with strongly differentiated text sizes layered between — a title, the main block, small bits below, the very top and bottom framing lines, and even some text over the image. Liked a lot; unexplored so far.

## What Buck likes

"I like this one a lot... I like the text framing at the top and the bottom and then you have even more differential varied sizes of text. There's the title, there's the fucking main block, and then there's some little bit down below. That's not even accounting for the very top and bottom shit and then there's even some shit over the image."

## Control for (locked)

- **Zones:** text framing at top and bottom · framing lines structural, not decoration
- **Type (scale range + size count):** dramatic differentials · four or more sizes · title / main block / small bits below / top-bottom framing text / text over image · the spread between sizes is the point
- **Grid (containment):** held · border, margin, padding "for sure" · not full bleed
