# 15 numbering system — expressions doc

**Version:** V1 · 7.12.26 · created from the board pass (Buck's board comment, worked by AI — Buck QAs)
**Type:** brand-wide system definition — not one composition but a rule that spans every expression that numbers anything. Middle-column candidate (the system kind, not the little-move kind).

## What it is

The numbering of things, defined as a system: Roman numerals or normal numerals, parentheses or not, leading zero or not. The requirement is consistency — one system applied across the whole brand — but which system is an open exploration with several candidates to try.

## What Buck likes

The systematization itself: "There's a bunch of different ways to do it and I wanna define that and kind of try some different systems. Systems meaning that it spans across the whole brand and it's consistent but there are different systems to apply and to take the approach and I wanna try different ones."

## Control for (locked)

- **Consistency:** whatever wins applies brand-wide. A page never mixes numbering systems.
- **The decision axes:** Roman vs. normal numerals · parentheses or not · leading zero or not (01 vs 1). These are the defined dimensions of the exploration.

## Leave open (variation space)

- Which system wins — the whole point. Try candidate systems across real surfaces: 09 roman numerals (Roman-numeral ordering), 14 folio rule (/05 sub-numbering), 16 tariff tables, and the offset-editorial parenthetical-numbering reference in band 15's own refs.
- Letter-level nuance once a system is picked: lining vs oldstyle numerals (ad logic vocabulary) — untouched by feedback but the natural next question.

## Negative

- Inconsistency — multiple systems live at once. That's the only kill.

## Open questions

- Whether a system decision lands in 01 settled/ once made (it has the shape of a settled item — flagged for when it's actually decided).
- Middle-column categorization — this one is a *system*, arguably a third kind next to compositions, little moves, and assets. Feeds the taxonomy read at the end of the pass.
