# 25 wordmark redraw — expressions doc

**Version:** V1 · 7.12.26 · created from the board pass (Buck's board comment, worked by AI — Buck QAs)
**Type:** graphic asset effort (right of the red line) — lettering; refine the existing neon-sign wordmark. Mirrored on 05 graphics/to-make.md.

## What it is

A redraw of the Hotel Paisano wordmark — refinement, not replacement. The mark comes directly from the neon sign on the building, and the sign is not changing; the identity's tie to the physical building holds. The script explorations of round one exposed the current mark's weaknesses without being its replacement: "the AI explorations... exposed weaknesses in the current logo and suggested a direction for refining it."

## What Buck likes / wants

The diagnosis: "It feels a little too digital, a little too glossy, a little too manufactured — like somebody drew it in Photoshop twenty years ago instead of it being a naturally drawn piece of lettering." The direction: "there's room to redraw the logo so it moves slightly toward the qualities these script explorations have. It shouldn't become the script, but it should become more natural, more fluid, and better drawn while still clearly feeling like the same sign." What must survive: "its overall structure, proportions, and little details like the tails on certain letters."

## Control for (locked) — graphic/lettering controls

- **Letterforms:** the neon sign's letterforms as the base · overall structure preserved · proportions preserved · letter tails preserved · the redraw lives inside them
- **Technique (hand):** naturally drawn over machine-glossy · more natural, more fluid, better drawn · kill the digital / glossy / manufactured / Photoshop-era read (audit fix: "tight hand" was an earlier draft's vocabulary pick, unsupported and in tension with "fluid" — the degree of hand is open)
- **Mark type / lockup:** unchanged · redraw the drawing, don't restructure the mark
- **The boundary (descriptive label, not a schema control):** never becomes the script · "still clearly feeling like the same sign" · the identity-to-building relationship holds

## Leave open (variation space)

- How far "slightly toward" the script qualities goes — degrees of fluidity to iterate.
- Weight, stroke behavior, edge quality at small sizes — production territory once iteration starts.

## Negative

- Becoming the script — the hard line, repeated: "I don't think it should become one of these scripts."
- Staying exactly as it is — "I also don't think it should stay exactly as it is."
- Conflating this with the website's oversized-script expression (01): "two separate decisions, and I don't think they should be conflated."

## Open questions

- The logo-script question remains formally OPEN per the standing rule — this doc records the current direction (refine, not replace), not a settled decision. Nothing here goes to 01 settled.
