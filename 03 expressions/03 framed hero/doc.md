# 03 framed hero — expressions doc

**Version:** V1 · 7.12.26 · created from the board pass (Buck's board comment, worked by AI — Buck QAs)
**Type:** composition concept — the hero section pattern, derived from the Bowery Hotels hero.

## What it is

The site hero, derived nearly straight from the Bowery Hotels hero section reference. The round-one explorations were faithful variants that didn't yet use the Paisano wordmark — which they must. The defining move against the reference's world: content held inside tight, thin padding or margins rather than running to the edges.

## What Buck likes

"There are a couple good ones here." The Bowery containment is the keeper: "I don't like full bleed all the way to the edges for this brand. I'd rather have it be where there's really tight thin padding or margins around the outside, kind of like Bowery."

## Control for (locked)

- **Grid (containment):** held — tight, thin padding/margins framing the hero on all sides. Never edge to edge.
- **Brand:** the actual Hotel Paisano wordmark in the hero — "it obviously should be [using our wordmark]. We don't fuck with the paisano." No stand-in faces.
- **Subject:** the site hero — the opening section of the page.

## Leave open (variation space)

- **Imagery:** hero image choice and conduct — fills from 04 media at production.
- **Type:** faces, sizing, casing (not landed brand-wide).
- **Navigation:** how the hero and nav share the frame — see 24 nav bar; unaddressed in the feedback.
- **Zones / Hierarchy / Space** within the framed hero — cycle.
- Carousel/slide behavior, if any — the reference implies one, feedback doesn't lock it.

## Negative

- No full bleed to the edges.
- No arrows inside circles for carousel controls: "the arrows inside the circles are retarded."
- No placeholder/foreign wordmark in place of ours.

## Open questions
