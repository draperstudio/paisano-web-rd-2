## Goal

Get travelers planning a Marfa trip to book the Paisano at $425 a night.

---

### EMPHASIS

Communications prioritize in this order:

**01 The place.** Photography and video of the hotel: the courtyard, the restaurant, the bar, the rooms, and the building at night. No competitor in Marfa can show anything equivalent. The place does the persuading before the copy does.

**02 The people and the operation.** The owners live in Marfa and run the hotel directly. Chef Brian Espinosa runs Meseta. The desk is staffed around the clock. These are operating facts that separate the Paisano from every other option in town.

**03 The building and its history.** The 1930 Trost building, the original tile, the neon, the courtyard. The architecture and provenance are real and documented. Giant is part of the history but not the lead; the building existed for 26 years before the film and has been operating for 70 years since.

**04 Practical information.** Room types, rates, hours, food schedule, pet policy, booking, and event capacity. This is what closes the decision. It should be easy to find, specific, and current.

---

### HOW PEOPLE FIND AND EVALUATE THE HOTEL

This is not a clean linear funnel. Different people enter at different points, skip stages, and circle back.

**Discovery is mostly visual.** People encounter the Paisano through photography on Instagram, in editorial coverage, in travel articles, or in a friend's post. The neon sign, the courtyard, and the building exterior are the most recognizable images. At this stage they are not comparing hotels. They are saving a place they want to visit.

**Trip planning is where comparison starts.** Once a Marfa trip is real, they look at options. The Paisano, the St. George, and short-term rentals are the main alternatives. They want to see rooms and bathrooms, understand the rate, and confirm that the hotel is actually renovated and not coasting on the old building.

**Practical questions decide the timing.** Can they bring the dog? Is there food on a Tuesday? Can they book one night? What is there to do besides Chinati Weekend? These questions determine whether a trip happens this month or gets postponed.

**Comparison is against their last Marfa stay.** Repeat visitors compare to the St. George, a rental they liked, or the old Paisano before renovation. First-time visitors compare to what $425 gets them in other cities.

**Booking needs to be direct and fast.** The hotel site, not a third-party platform. One-night stays as easy to book as multi-night. Event inquiries answered by a real person quickly.

## Content Approach

Paisano content leads with the place. It opens on the courtyard in the evening, a table at Meseta, the neon against the sky, or a room with the shutters open. The photograph does the persuading, and the words around it stay short.

The register is documentary. The brand shows what is actually there and says what it actually knows: names, dates, hours, routes, and prices. It does not sell, and it does not use marketing language.

The hotel covers the town, and not only itself. Chinati, Ballroom, the theater, the neighboring businesses, and the region's ranches are all subject matter. The Paisano can do this credibly because it is the building at the center of town, and content that helps someone plan a Marfa trip serves the hotel even when the hotel is not the subject.

History is working material, spent in steady doses. Trost, Giant, and the original details appear as single stories told well, spread across many pieces rather than emptied all at once.

One subject produces content at several depths. The same shoot yields a single image, a practical guide, and a longer story, and reporting is planned so it feeds all three.

---

## Editorial Angles

- **Scene.** A single moment at the hotel or in town, shown with little or no text: the courtyard at dusk, the lobby in the morning, and the neon at night.
- **Guide.** Practical answers to real trip questions: when to come, what is open, how to do Chinati Weekend, and which room fits which trip.
- **Archive.** The building's and the town's history told from primary material: Giant, Trost, old photographs, and the original details.
- **Profile.** A person, told straight: the chef, a rancher behind the menu, a staff member, or a neighbor.
- **Process.** Work in progress on the property: a room coming back, the kitchen taking shape, and the trades doing the work.
- **Calendar.** What is happening and when: the art calendar, events at the hotel, and the town's seasonal rhythm.

---

## Web Funnel

- **Feel.** The page opens on the place at its best, the courtyard or the neon at night, before any words.
- **Orient.** One passage states what this is: the landmark hotel at the center of Marfa, with rooms, food, and events.
- **Show.** The rooms by type, the restaurant, the courtyard, and the spaces, in photography.
- **Ground.** The facts: rates, hours, the six room types, policies, and the history.
- **Resolve.** Dates, direct booking, and event inquiries, with nothing in the way.

## Social Funnel

### Awareness

**Their mindset:** They are not looking for a hotel. They follow Marfa, West Texas, art, food, and design, and they save places they want to feel someday.

**Content's Job:** Make them want to be at the Paisano before they have a trip planned.

**What Goes Here:**

- Scenes of the hotel: courtyard evenings, the lobby, the neon
- West Texas routes and the drive itself
- Giant and the hotel's Hollywood history
- Chinati, Ballroom, and the art calendar
- Desert food and the ranches behind it

### Consideration

**Their mindset:** They are planning a Marfa trip and comparing options. They want to see the rooms, know what is open, and decide whether the rate is fair.

**Content's Job:** Answer the trip questions and show the rooms plainly.

**What Goes Here:**

- Room tours by type, including the bathrooms
- Guides: when to come, what is open, how many nights
- The restaurant, the menu, and the seven-day food schedule
- The courtyard and the Texas building for weddings and events
- Dogs, one-night stays, and the practical policies

### Conversion

**Their mindset:** They are ready and practical. The remaining questions are dates, availability, and how to hold what they want.

**Content's Job:** Make booking direct and immediate.

**What Goes Here:**

- Seasonal windows and high-demand weekends, stated early
- Direct booking with live availability
- Event and block inquiries with a real contact
- Which room to pick for the trip they described
