# Context Tray Package

## Strategic Brief

## Task

Design and build the Hotel Paisano launch website, live before June 21, 2025. This is the hotel's first digital presence under new ownership. Takeover is July 1. The site must facilitate bookings, introduce the new brand, and manage expectations during a transitional period where the brand is fresh but the physical product (rooms especially) is not yet renovated. Restaurant renovation begins this summer; rooms follow in January 2027.

---

## Who This Is For

People who are already planning or considering a Marfa trip. They are not discovering Marfa here. They are comparing where to stay, confirming practical details, and deciding whether to book.

The primary segments:

- **Art-trip couples** from Austin, Dallas, Houston, LA, and New York, planning around Chinati Weekend, galleries, and the cultural calendar. Many have been before and stayed at the St. George or a rental. They will evaluate the Paisano against that experience and against the rate.
- **West Texas road-trippers** driving the Big Bend circuit, often midweek, often with a dog. Marfa is one or two nights inside a longer trip. They need to know the practical things work (food, pet policy, late arrival).
- **Wedding and event planners** evaluating whether the property can handle a ceremony, dinner, and room block on one site. Their timeline is months out and their questions are logistical.

All of them will click "Book Now" and land in an external PMS booking portal we do not control. That portal handles room-type detail, photo carousels, and availability. The site does not need to replicate that information.

---

## Who We Are (Right Now)

A new ownership group taking over a 1930 Henry Trost landmark at the center of Marfa. We have a fresh brand, a vision, a chef (Brian Espinosa for La Meseta), and a renovation plan. We do not yet have renovated rooms, updated room photography, or a finished restaurant. The lobby and common areas are in better shape than the rooms.

We are the historic building at the center of town, under new stewardship, with a clear plan and a timeline. We are not yet the finished product.

---

## What We Are Offering (On This Site, At This Stage)

The site is not selling the renovated Paisano. It is selling:

1. **The building and its presence.** The 1930 Trost architecture, the courtyard, the neon, and the lobby. No other hotel in Marfa has anything comparable. This is the visual asset we can actually show well right now.
2. **A place to stay in Marfa now, with honest expectations.** 48 rooms, a front desk, operational basics. Not luxury at this stage, but a functioning hotel in the landmark building at the center of town.
3. **Local context that makes the trip better.** A curated Marfa guide that helps plan the visit. This is the most immediately useful content the site can offer, and it positions the Paisano as the town's base.
4. **The story of what is coming.** New ownership, a renovation plan with a timeline, and future F&B concepts (La Meseta, Blue Bar, and Cafe Paisano). These are part of the revitalization narrative, not standalone offerings yet.
5. **Practical booking access.** CTA routes to the PMS portal. Direct and fast, with no friction on our side.

---

## What We Are Messaging

**Primary message:** The Paisano is back. New ownership, same landmark. The hotel is open now and being restored to what it should be.

**Supporting messages:**

- The building is a 1930 Trost landmark with the courtyard, the lobby, and the neon. No other hotel in Marfa has any of it.
- We are investing seriously: new restaurant, new rooms, new amenities, with a public timeline.
- You can stay here now. The hotel is open and operational through the entire renovation.
- We are not overpromising. What is ready is ready, and what is coming is clearly marked as coming.

---

## Why This Approach

The core constraint is the gap between brand quality and room quality. The brand strategy says the place does the persuading before the copy does, and that room photos and bathroom photos decide whether people trust the hotel. Right now, we cannot lead with rooms. So the site has to lead with what we do have (the building, the common spaces, the vision) and be transparent about what we do not.

"Show less, but better" is the operating principle. The site should feel intentional and restrained, not empty. If we cannot show something well, we do not show it.

The PMS portal already handles the functional booking detail (room types, photos, and availability). The site does not need to duplicate that, especially when the room photography would undermine the brand. Instead, the site communicates room inventory at a high level (types, general description) and routes to the PMS for specifics.

Content priority should follow what is real and useful to the visitor today, not what we are excited about internally. The Marfa guide is the highest-value content the site can offer right now because it helps every visitor regardless of whether the hotel is renovated. The F&B concepts, rooms, and amenities all improve over time as the renovation progresses. They belong in the revitalization narrative, mentioned clearly and specifically, but they do not warrant standalone sections for things that do not exist yet.

---

## What the Site Needs to Do

1. **Establish the brand.** First impression of the new Paisano. The tone and visual identity of the new ownership need to come through on the first page.
2. **Facilitate bookings.** Clear CTA to the PMS portal. Visitors should never wonder how to book.
3. **Provide local context.** A Marfa guide with curated recommendations: where to eat, what to see, the art calendar, Big Bend and Fort Davis as part of the same trip, and practical information like what is open midweek. This is the most useful thing the site offers a visitor planning a trip right now. It gives the site real depth and positions the Paisano as the town's base.
4. **Tell the building's story.** History page with the Trost architecture, the Giant connection, and the hotel's place in Marfa. No competitor has anything equivalent.
5. **Manage the transition honestly.** Acknowledge new ownership, the renovation plan, and the timeline. Reassure that the hotel is open. Do not hide the work in progress. Present it straightforwardly as part of the story. The F&B concepts (La Meseta, Blue Bar, and Cafe Paisano), room renovations, and amenity plans all live here, named and described with enough specificity to be credible, but clearly framed as what is coming rather than what is available now.
6. **Open the events channel.** Contact pathway for weddings, buyouts, and group inquiries. Not a full portal, just a clear way into a conversation.

---

## Design and UX Considerations

The site should not look or feel like a standard hotel website (hero image, booking bar, alternating image/text sections, room-type carousel). The brand is new and the property is in transition. The site should reflect both of those things.

**Key tensions to manage in structure:**

- The homepage should feel like a statement, not a brochure. Exploring a locked-frame landing (Chateau Marmont and Bowery Hotel references) with a watercolor or painted rendering of the building rather than photography, to set tone without showing rooms.
- Rooms and amenities do not currently warrant their own full pages. The information is thin and the photography is weak. But visitors need to know what is available. High-level room inventory information (types, general description) and current amenities belong inside the revitalization page, where the context of "here is what we have now and here is what is coming" makes the most sense.
- The F&B concepts are not open. They should be named and described inside the revitalization narrative, not given their own section. When they open, they earn their own pages.
- The Marfa guide should be one of the most prominent destinations on the site. It is the content with the most immediate value to every visitor segment, and it is the one area where the site can be genuinely rich right now without running into the photography or renovation constraints.
- The revitalization story needs its own space. It could be a dedicated page linked from a homepage callout. This is where the transparency lives and where future updates can be posted as the renovation progresses.
- Contact and events can share a page or section. The need is simple: get people into a conversation.

**Navigation should be lean.** The site does not have enough finished content to support a deep structure. A few clear destinations, not a dozen tabs.

---

## Desired Outcome

A visitor planning a Marfa trip lands on the site and understands three things quickly: this is the landmark building at the center of town, it is under serious new ownership with a real plan, and they can book a room now. The site reads as confident and deliberate. They are not oversold. They book, or they bookmark and come back when the renovation is further along.
