# Controls - Composition

Pulled from Notion (AD-CD-Controls-Logic > Controls > Controls - Composition) on 7.11.26.

## Base (All Types)

### Upper Level

| Control | What to Address in Snippet Direction |
|---|---|
| **Grid** | The framework the layout is built on. How many columns it implies and whether the structure shows or stays invisible. What aligns to what, and how strictly the edges hold the line. How wide the content sits within the frame, running edge to edge or held in a column. Where the layout breaks its own grid, since a break only reads as deliberate when the rest holds. |
| **Space** | How tight or open the layout sits. The room around and between elements, and whether it stays even or shifts from section to section. How packed or airy the whole surface reads. The rhythm the spacing creates as the eye moves down or across. Whether the space feels generous and calm or dense and urgent, since spacing is the fastest read on how expensive a layout feels. |
| **Type** | The scale of the text across the layout. The range from the largest heading down to the smallest print, and how far apart the sizes sit. How many distinct sizes are in play. Whether the jumps between sizes are dramatic or gentle, and what that does to how loud the page speaks. Whether the smallest text still reads at the size and distance the surface is actually seen. The sizing itself, not the typeface, which is set by the brand. |
| **Imagery** | How much the layout leans on pictures, video, or graphics. Whether imagery carries the surface or sits in support of the text. How big the images run and how many there are. How the imagery is contained, running to the edges, held in frames, or cut out and floating. What the balance between image and text says about who the layout is for. |
| **Color** | The palette working across the composition. Which colors are in play and how many. The split between background, text, and accent, and which jobs each color holds. Whether the surface sits light or dark overall. How much of the work color is doing, whether it sets the structure or just marks the moments that matter. Where color concentrates and where it rests. |
| **Brand** | How present the brand is in the frame. Where the logo and brand marks sit and the size they take. How hard they assert against the rest of the layout, leading the surface or signing it quietly in a corner. Whether brand shows up only as marks or also through owned colors, shapes, and patterns. How early the viewer should know whose layout this is. |
| **Mood** | The overall personality of the composition, how the surface feels before any of it gets read. The art direction intent for its tone and energy, whether it reads calm or urgent, premium or approachable, strict or playful. This is the target that the other upper-level controls should serve. |

### Lower Level

| Control | What to Address in the Snippet |
|---|---|
| **Subject** | What kind of surface it is and the job it exists to do. The dimensions it has to work within and where it will be seen. How much content has to fit on it and how loaded or sparse that makes it. What the content actually is, the text, images, and pieces the layout has to hold. Whether everything carries equal standing or some of it is clearly there in support. |
| **Hierarchy** | What the viewer takes in first, second, and last. How that order is set, through size, weight, contrast, and position. Where the lead element sits and how far it stands above the rest. How steep the ranking is, one thing clearly on top or several competing evenly. What gets deliberately played down so the important parts can lead. |
| **Balance** | How the visual weight is distributed across the frame. Whether it sits even and symmetrical or is deliberately pushed to one side. How the empty space plays into that balance, holding its own against the filled areas. Whether the frame feels stable or tense, and whether that tension is doing work. Top against bottom as much as left against right, since layouts are mostly read downward. |
| **Message** | What the viewer should take away and what they should do next. The single point the composition is built to land, said in one line. How directly the surface pushes toward the action, a hard ask or a quiet suggestion. This is the target that the other lower-level controls should serve. |

## Specific Types

### Web Design
*A page or section of a website*

| Control | What to Address in the Snippet |
|---|---|
| **Navigation** | How the wayfinding is handled. Where the menu lives in the frame and what form it takes, a full bar, a few links, or tucked behind a button. How present or hidden it is and what that says about how much exploring the page expects. Whether it holds in place as the page moves or scrolls away with it. How the visitor always knows where they are and where they can go. |
| **CTA** | The actions the page is driving toward. Which action is the main one and where it sits in the frame. How many asks the page makes and whether they compete or line up behind the lead. How hard the main action stands out from everything around it, through color, size, and the room it gets. Whether secondary actions are offered quietly or not at all. |
| **Zones** | How the frame is divided into regions. How many zones there are and how they split the surface. How distinct each zone reads from the next, separated by space alone or by visible change. Whether the zones repeat a shared module, the same card again and again, or each zone is its own design. Whether the zones run in one column or sit side by side. The order the zones are meant to be read in and whether the layout makes that order obvious. |
| **Dividers** | The lines and rules that separate one part of the layout from another. Where they fall and how heavy they read. Whether separation comes from drawn lines, from shifts in background, or from space alone. Whether the dividers do structural work or sit as surface detail. How the divider style repeats, since one inconsistent break reads as a mistake. |

### Social Media
*Posts, carousels, story slides*

| Control | What to Address in the Snippet |
|---|---|
| **Overlay** | How text and image sit together when one is laid over the other. Where the words sit against the image and how the two share the frame. How the image beneath is treated so the words stay readable, darkened, blurred, or backed with a solid shape. How far that treatment is pushed before the image stops mattering. Whether the words and the image say the same thing or play against each other. |
| **Accents** | The small decorative marks added on top of the core content. What they are, stickers, scribbles, arrows, shapes, or hand-drawn touches. How many there are and where they cluster. How much they shape the character of the frame, a light garnish or half the personality. Whether they point at something, since an accent that directs the eye is doing more than decorating. |
| **Safezone** | The parts of the frame the platform crops or covers with its own interface. Where those areas fall for the format this piece is made for. How the layout keeps the important content clear of them. How much room it holds at the edges so nothing critical sits under the controls. What is allowed to run into the risky areas, since backgrounds can take the loss but words and faces cannot. |

### Slide Deck
*Individual slides that present a narrative, data, or argument in sequence*

| Control | What to Address in the Snippet |
|---|---|
| **Data** | How data is presented and laid out. The form it takes, a chart, a table, a few big numbers, or a single figure standing alone. How dense the information reads and how much the audience is asked to take in at once. Which figure is pulled forward as the takeaway and how the slide makes it impossible to miss. Whether the data is there to prove the point or to be explored. |
| **Background Color** | How a background color is used to set a slide apart or push a point. How much of the frame it covers, a full flood or a held panel. How far it departs from the rest of the deck and what that break signals, a new chapter, a big claim, or a moment to slow down. How the content on top adjusts so it still reads against the changed ground. |

## New Controls — Not Yet Vetted

Folded in from Erik's 7.11 analysis of one-shot prompt sites, per the 7.11 call ("this just needs to be folded into the control set and the AD logic"). The names and the split between these three are not settled — Motion and Time may be one control. Vet during the board pass.

| Control | What to Address in the Snippet |
|---|---|
| **Depth** | What sits in front of what. Layering, overlap, shadow, and parallax. Whether the surface reads as one flat plane or as stacked layers, and how far the stack goes. Whether depth comes from drawn cues like shadow and overlap or from movement like parallax. What the layering is doing, separating content from ground, holding a fixed element over a changing one, or pure atmosphere. |
| **Motion** | Whether the surface moves at all, and what moves against what stays still. Who controls time: plays on its own on a timeline, fires once when a threshold is crossed, or is bound to the person's scroll or cursor and runs both ways. How the motion reads through its easing, responsive, composed, or mechanical. How many moving systems run at once, since restraint is what keeps motion readable. What the static fallback is when motion is off or reduced. |
| **Time** | Pacing and sequence in media that unfold. What is revealed when, what is held back, and how the reveal is paced down the scroll or timeline. Whether each step of the unfolding gives the person something new. Where the piece slows down and where it lets the person run. |
