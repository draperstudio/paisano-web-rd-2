## Composition Nuance to Work In
(Jack, 7.11 — came from working out what to hone in on to make variations; informs these controls, not yet worked into the tables below)

**Scale cliff** — the ratio between the largest and smallest element, and *where* the drop happens. A 12rem word next to 11px mono is a different room than everything at medium.
**Axis & void placement** — centered vs. offset; whether the emptiness pools top, left, or around; optical center vs. geometric center.
**Type voice-switching** — *which single word* gets the script; mid-line register changes; caps vs. italic; where the weight lands.
**Letter-level nuance** — tracking on the small caps, leading tightness on display lines, whether numerals are lining or oldstyle.
**Image conduct** — crop tightness, aspect, size relative to the field, whether it bleeds, floats, or sits inset; how *few* you can hold.
**Whitespace distribution** — symmetrical breathing vs. lopsided silence. This is composition, not padding.
**Rhythm** — one element vs. a trio; cadence of repetition down the page.
**Line work** — hairlines as *punctuation* (a rule under a label), never as containers. No boxes.
**Accent deployment** — where the one hit of oxblood lands and how rarely.
**Density gradient** — where information gathers and where it thins to nothing.
**Eye path** — Z-sweep, vertical drop, or orbit around a pinned object.
**The withheld thing** — what each variation refuses to show.

## Upper Level

### Grid

**Framework**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Single Column** | Everything stacks in one column down the page. The reading order is the layout, top to bottom with no decisions left to the viewer. Feels focused, linear, and easy. |
| **Multi-Column** | Content splits into two, three, or four columns sharing the width. The surface organizes side by side and the eye reads across as well as down. Feels structured, efficient, and editorial. |
| **Modular** | The surface divides into repeating cells, the same unit over and over. Content snaps into modules and the system shows. Feels systematic, scalable, and engineered. |
| **Asymmetric** | Columns of deliberately unequal width split the surface, usually around two thirds against one third. One side carries the weight and the other answers it. Feels modern, designed, and intentional. |
| **Freeform** | No underlying structure at all. Elements sit where the eye wants them, placed by feel. Feels loose, expressive, and handmade. |

**Strictness**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Locked** | Every element snaps to the structure with no exceptions. Edges align perfectly everywhere the eye checks. Feels disciplined, calm, and trustworthy. |
| **Mostly Held** | The structure holds but small departures appear where content needs them. Order with signs of life. Feels professional, natural, and easy. |
| **Loose** | The grid is a suggestion the layout keeps half-following. Alignment comes and goes. Feels casual, relaxed, and human. |
| **Broken** | The structure exists so specific elements can violate it. The breaks carry the energy and everything else holds still. Feels bold, deliberate, and charged. |

**Containment**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Edge to Edge** | Content runs the full width with no margins holding it back. The surface uses everything it has. Feels immersive, big, and confident. |
| **Wide** | Content sits in a broad column with slim margins. Generous but still framed. Feels open, contemporary, and comfortable. |
| **Held** | Content sits in a clearly contained column with real margins on both sides. The emptiness frames the work. Feels composed, premium, and controlled. |
| **Narrow** | Content holds a slim column inside a much wider frame. Most of the surface is margin. Feels focused, exclusive, and quiet. |

**Alignment**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Left** | Everything hangs from a hard left edge. Lines start in the same place and reading is frictionless. Feels natural, steady, and professional. |
| **Centered** | Elements balance around the middle axis. The layout faces the viewer head on. Feels classic, composed, and presentational. |
| **Right** | Elements hang from the right edge against reading habit. The unusual anchor draws notice. Feels deliberate, styled, and unexpected. |
| **Mixed** | Different sections take different anchors on purpose. The shifts mark chapters and create movement. Feels dynamic, editorial, and designed. |

### Space

**Density**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Packed** | Elements run close together with minimal gaps. The surface fills and the energy is high. Feels urgent, abundant, and loud. |
| **Comfortable** | Standard breathing room between everything. Nothing fights and nothing floats. Feels balanced, friendly, and invisible. |
| **Airy** | Spacing runs well past comfortable. Elements stand apart and the pauses get long. Feels calm, premium, and unhurried. |
| **Sparse** | Vast emptiness with content as small islands. The space dominates the surface. Feels exclusive, still, and expensive. |

**Rhythm**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Steady** | The same spacing repeats all the way down. The eye settles into the beat and moves without thinking. Feels consistent, reliable, and calm. |
| **Sectioned** | Spacing shifts between sections, tight inside groups and wide between them. The gaps do the organizing. Feels structured, clear, and paced. |
| **Punctuated** | Long tight runs broken by sudden big pauses. The pauses land like beats and reset the eye. Feels dramatic, editorial, and intentional. |

### Type

**Scale Range**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Flat (1.1-1.2)** | All text sizes sit close together, each step only a tenth to a fifth larger than the one below. Nothing shouts and the surface reads as one even voice. Feels modest, dense, and bookish. |
| **Moderate (1.25-1.333)** | Clear steps between heading and body, each size a quarter to a third larger than the one below. Hierarchy reads instantly and politely. Feels professional, balanced, and standard. |
| **Dramatic (1.5-1.618)** | Big jumps from huge to small, each step half again the size below or more. Headlines own the surface and small text becomes texture. Feels confident, loud, and contemporary. |
| **Extreme (2.0 and up)** | Sizes double or more between steps, and the largest type approaches the size of the frame while the smallest nearly disappears. Type becomes image. Feels monumental, graphic, and fearless. |

**Size Count**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Two Sizes** | One size leads and one size carries everything else. The system could not be simpler. Feels minimal, strict, and confident. |
| **Three Sizes** | Lead, support, and detail, each one step down. The standard kit that covers most jobs. Feels organized, natural, and complete. |
| **Four or More** | A full ladder of sizes for layered content. Rich hierarchy with more decisions on display. Feels editorial, busy, and informative. |

### Imagery

**Share**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **None** | No imagery at all, type and color carry the whole surface. Every job pictures would do, words do. Feels typographic, direct, and confident. |
| **Supporting** | Pictures appear small and contained while text leads. Images illustrate, they never take over. Feels informative, modest, and text-first. |
| **Equal** | Image and text split the surface evenly. Both carry weight and the eye moves between them. Feels balanced, magazine-like, and rich. |
| **Image-Led** | Pictures dominate and text rides on top or alongside. The image sets the mood before a word is read. Feels visual, emotional, and immersive. |
| **Image-Only** | The picture is the layout. Whatever text exists is minimal and stays out of the way. Feels cinematic, bold, and wordless. |

**Containment**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Full Bleed** | Images run past the margins to the edges of the frame. No border separates picture from surface. Feels immersive, modern, and seamless. |
| **Framed** | Images sit in defined boxes with the grid visible around them. Order holds the picture. Feels structured, editorial, and curated. |
| **Cutout** | Subjects are isolated from their backgrounds and float directly on the surface. Objects feel placed by hand. Feels playful, light, and product-like. |
| **Background** | The image sits behind everything as the ground the layout lives on. Content floats above it. Feels atmospheric, layered, and deep. |

### Color

**Background**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Light** | A white or near-white surface with dark content on top. The default of print and the web, maximum readability. Feels open, honest, and everyday. |
| **Dark** | A black or near-black surface with light content. Contrast flips and everything on it gains drama. Feels premium, technical, and night-side. |
| **Colored** | A hue takes the ground and the whole surface becomes that color. The layout commits before any content loads. Feels branded, bold, and ownable. |
| **Split** | Different zones take different grounds. The color changes mark the chapters. Feels dynamic, sectioned, and designed. |

**Color Load**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Neutral** | No active color anywhere, just blacks, whites, and grays. Structure and type do all the talking. Feels serious, restrained, and timeless. |
| **Accent** | Neutrals carry the surface and one color appears only at the moments that matter. The accent points and the rest stays quiet. Feels focused, disciplined, and sharp. |
| **Structural** | Color separates zones, marks roles, and organizes the surface. The layout reads through its colors. Feels systematic, lively, and clear. |
| **Flooded** | Color covers everything and the surface swims in it. The palette is the experience. Feels immersive, loud, and unmistakable. |

**Hierarchy**

| Role | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Dominant (40-60%)** | The color holding the most area, usually the ground. Sets the temperature of the whole surface and is the first thing the viewer registers. |
| **Supporting (20-30%)** | Bridges the dominant and the accents. Carries secondary zones and connects the parts of the layout. |
| **Accent (5-10%)** | The smallest and most noticed color. Marks the actions and moments the viewer must not miss, usually the most saturated thing on the surface. |
| **Neutral Foundation (20-30%)** | Whites, grays, and blacks with no strong hue. Carry text and give the active colors room to work. |

### Brand

**Presence**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Signature** | A small mark in a corner, the way a painter signs a canvas. The work leads and the brand confirms. Feels confident, quiet, and assured. |
| **Present** | The brand is clearly visible without leading. Marks and brand color appear where the eye passes. Feels professional, steady, and known. |
| **Prominent** | The brand leads the surface. The mark runs large and the layout is unmistakably theirs before any content reads. Feels assertive, proud, and loud. |
| **Saturated** | Brand everywhere, marks, colors, shapes, and patterns covering the surface. The layout is the brand. Feels owned, immersive, and total. |

**Placement**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Corner** | The mark holds a corner, out of the content's way but always found. Feels standard, tidy, and expected. |
| **Header** | The mark anchors a bar across the top. The brand opens the page the way a sign opens a store. Feels structural, official, and established. |
| **Centered** | The mark sits on the layout's center axis and becomes a formal element of the composition. Feels ceremonial, classic, and important. |
| **Repeated** | The mark recurs across the surface as a pattern or watermark. Ownership through repetition. Feels branded, decorative, and deliberate. |

### Mood

| Option | What It Feels Like |
|---|---|
| **Calm** | Generous space, steady rhythm, flat scale jumps, and a light or neutral ground. Nothing competes and nothing hurries. Feels quiet, assured, and easy. |
| **Urgent** | Packed density, steep hierarchy, hard contrast, and accents shouting. The surface pushes the viewer to act now. Feels pressing, loud, and fast. |
| **Premium** | Airy to sparse spacing, held column, restrained color, and small confident branding. Cost reads through restraint. Feels expensive, composed, and exclusive. |
| **Approachable** | Comfortable density, rounded moments, warm accents, and modest type jumps. The surface lowers its shoulders. Feels friendly, open, and safe. |
| **Strict** | Locked grid, hard alignment, neutral color, and exact spacing throughout. The system is the personality. Feels disciplined, precise, and serious. |
| **Playful** | Broken grid moments, mixed alignment, bright accents, and scattered imagery. The rules bend visibly. Feels light, fun, and loose. |
| **Bold** | Extreme type scale, edge-to-edge content, flooded color, and absolute hierarchy. The layout commits everywhere. Feels fearless, direct, and big. |
| **Editorial** | Multi-column structure, punctuated rhythm, dramatic type, and framed imagery. The surface reads like a publication. Feels curated, literate, and considered. |
| **Technical** | Modular grid, flat scale, neutral palette, and visible structure. The layout looks measured because it is. Feels precise, engineered, and credible. |
| **Warm** | Light ground with warm accents, comfortable spacing, and image-led moments. The surface radiates toward the viewer. Feels human, inviting, and close. |
| **Energetic** | Tipped balance, saturated accents, tight rhythm, and movement in the structure. The surface vibrates. Feels alive, fast, and charged. |
| **Quiet** | Sparse density, narrow containment, neutral load, and signature branding. Almost nothing, placed perfectly. Feels still, minimal, and certain. |
| **Corporate** | Locked grid, neutral or blue-leaning palette, moderate type scale, and the brand present at every turn. Nothing risks anything. Feels official, institutional, and dependable. |
| **Raw** | Default-looking type, hard edges, no decoration, and structure exposed like scaffolding. The layout refuses to perform. Feels blunt, confrontational, and real. |
| **Retro** | Period type scale, centered formality, ornament, and the spacing habits of another decade. The surface belongs to a time on purpose. Feels nostalgic, charming, and knowing. |
| **Futuristic** | Dark ground, modular structure, glowing accents, and tight engineered type. The surface points past the present. Feels advanced, sleek, and confident. |

## Lower Level

### Hierarchy

**Steepness**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Flat** | Everything carries the same weight. No element outranks another and the viewer chooses their own path. Feels democratic, calm, and catalog-like. |
| **Gentle** | A lead exists but the gap to everything else stays small. Order without pressure. Feels balanced, respectful, and easy. |
| **Steep** | One element clearly on top, everything else visibly behind it. The viewer's first move is decided for them. Feels directed, confident, and clear. |
| **Absolute** | One element is the layout and the rest is footnote. Nothing competes, by design. Feels dramatic, singular, and unmissable. |

**Lead Position**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Top** | The lead sits first in reading order at the top of the surface. The layout answers immediately and details follow. Feels conventional, efficient, and dependable. |
| **Center** | The lead holds the middle with everything orbiting it. The eye lands in the heart of the frame. Feels formal, focal, and presentational. |
| **Off-Center** | The lead sits deliberately to one side with space answering it. The placement itself is styled. Feels modern, composed, and editorial. |
| **Edge** | The lead pushes against or past the frame's edge. Tension comes from what almost leaves the layout. Feels daring, cropped, and energetic. |

### Balance

**Distribution**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Even** | Weight spreads equally across the frame in both directions. The surface sits still and settled. Feels stable, formal, and trustworthy. |
| **Tipped** | Weight deliberately leans to one side with emptiness answering it. The imbalance is held, not accidental. Feels dynamic, modern, and styled. |
| **Top-Heavy** | The weight loads at the top and the surface lightens going down. Strong opening, quiet exit. Feels front-loaded, bold, and declarative. |
| **Bottom-Anchored** | The weight settles at the bottom and the top stays open. The layout grounds itself like a landscape. Feels rooted, calm, and solid. |

### Structure

The named ways to arrange a whole piece. A piece usually leads with one. Overlaps with the Grid Framework options above — reconcile when vetting.

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Single Focal Image** | One dominant image or object holds the surface. A poster, an album cover, a web hero, and an opening film shot. |
| **Modular Grid** | Uniform repeating cells. A contact sheet, a product catalog, a magazine grid, and an app gallery. |
| **Editorial Grid** | Elements placed off-grid with uneven gaps. A magazine spread, a zine, and an editorial web page. Signals curation. |
| **Split** | The surface divided into parts held in tension. A diptych, a before and after, and a split screen. |
| **Sequence** | The piece unfolds in ordered parts. Book pages, a slide deck, comic panels, film cuts, and a scrolling page. |
| **Collage** | Overlap, raw type, and deliberate crowding. A poster, a zine, and a brutalist web page. Signals attitude. |

### Motion Driver

The first motion decision: who controls time.

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Timer-Driven** | Plays on a fixed timeline, on load or on a schedule. Film, motion graphics, and an entrance sequence. The maker controls time. |
| **Trigger-Driven** | Plays once when a threshold is crossed, such as an element appearing when it enters view. Cheap and one-directional. |
| **Input-Driven (Scrubbed)** | Bound to the person's input such as scroll or cursor, so it runs both ways. Only on interactive surfaces, and the strongest engagement. |

### Easing

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Ease-Out** | Fast start, soft landing. Feels responsive. |
| **Ease-In-Out** | Soft both ends. Feels composed. |
| **Linear** | Constant rate. Feels mechanical. Correct only for scrubbed motion, where the input supplies the acceleration. |

### Motion Conduct

- **Choreography**: several properties driven from one progress value, so a sequence reads as one designed event instead of separate effects.
- **Stagger**: the same animation offset across elements by 0.1 to 0.2 seconds, so they arrive in sequence.
- **Restraint**: move only what should be looked at, keep one moving system at a time, and on screens respect the reduced-motion setting.

### Perception Mechanisms

How visual perception works, and how to use it. Documented effects, not house inventions. These inform choosing options across all the controls.

| Lever | How perception works | How to use it |
|---|---|---|
| Size | People look at the largest element first | Make the most important element clearly larger, and keep the rest quiet |
| Isolation | Space around an element makes it read as important | Give the priority element room instead of making it louder |
| Motion | Movement draws the eye involuntarily | Move only what should be looked at |
| Pattern interruption | People skim repetition and notice the exception | Set a consistent rhythm, then break it at the message |
| Bleed and crop | An image running off the frame implies more beyond it | Bleed images off the edge and crop subjects to add energy |
| Scanning patterns | In left-to-right languages the eye tends to enter top-left and exit bottom-right | Put entry information top-left and the action bottom-right |
| Progressive disclosure | People stay engaged when each step gives them something new | Reveal in steps, and pace it so each step delivers something |
| Direct manipulation | Interfaces that respond to input hold attention longer | Bind animation to cursor or scroll instead of timers |
| Persistent elements | The eye uses a fixed point to stay oriented across a change of scene | Keep one element fixed while the rest changes behind it |
| Value | Dark reads as heavy and premium, light reads as open and fast | Switch background value between sections to mark a change |
| Faces | People look at faces first and follow their gaze | Point a pictured face toward what should be seen next |

### Web Execution Vocabulary

The terminology layer for screens and web — the keywords, terms, and phrases for writing motion and interaction into build prompts. Print, packaging, and spatial work have their own execution vocabularies, to be added.

- **Scroll spacer**: an invisible tall element that gives the browser scroll distance while visible content stays fixed. Scroll position becomes a 0 to 1 value that drives the scenes. Also called virtual scroll.
- **Scrubbing**: setting a video or animation's progress manually from cursor or scroll position instead of letting it play.
- **Dead zone**: a buffer range around an input threshold where nothing changes, to stop flicker near the threshold.
- **Transform origin**: the anchor point a scale or rotation grows from. It shapes how an element appears to move.
- **Mapping**: converting one range to another, such as cursor position to video time. Most scrubbed effects are a mapping.
- **Blend modes**: CSS mix-blend-mode combines an element's color with what is behind it. Exclusion and difference invert against the background, keeping one white element legible over both light and dark.
- **Aspect-ratio cells with object-fit cover**: boxes locked to a fixed proportion, media cropped to fill. Uniform proportions keep a scattered layout intentional.
- **Generative layout**: element placement computed by an algorithm instead of by hand. An editorial look at scale.
- **Entrance stagger**: the load-time version of a stagger, usually opacity plus a small upward move.
- **Pinning**: fixing an element to the viewport for a scroll range, then releasing it. The basis of sticky stacks and scrollytelling.
- **Parallax**: layers moving at different rates under one scroll input, creating depth.
- **Throttling**: capping how often a repeating trigger fires, such as a scroll-driven randomizer at one change per 80 milliseconds.
- **requestAnimationFrame loop**: a function the browser calls before every repaint, about 60 times a second. It reads input, computes progress, and writes transforms. All smooth input-driven motion runs on it.
- **will-change**: a CSS hint that an element is about to animate, so the browser prepares a GPU layer and the motion does not stutter.
- **Loading gate**: holding content at zero opacity until its media is ready, then fading it in.
- **Custom cursor**: hiding the native cursor and replacing it with a page element that follows the mouse.
- **Graceful degradation**: a fallback for every input-driven effect. Cursor scrubbing needs autoplay on touch, and motion needs a static state under reduced-motion.
