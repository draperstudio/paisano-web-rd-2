## Upper Level

### Technique

**Medium**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Vector** | Built from mathematically clean paths and fills. Curves are perfect, color is perfectly even, and the same file scales to any size without losing a thing. Feels modern, exact, and professional. |
| **3D Render** | Built in software as objects with light falling on them. Surfaces carry highlights, shadows, and reflections that obey physics, and depth reads as real space. Feels dimensional, produced, and contemporary. |
| **Pixel Art** | Drawn square by square on a visible grid. Edges step instead of curve and detail is rationed pixel by pixel. Feels digital, nostalgic, and game-like. |
| **Ink** | Drawn by hand with pen or brush. Lines carry pressure, speed, and small wobbles, and no two marks are identical. Feels human, direct, and alive. |
| **Pencil** | Drawn in graphite with visible stroke buildup. Tone comes from layered strokes, edges stay soft, and the paper shows through. Feels sketched, intimate, and in-progress. |
| **Charcoal** | Drawn in soft black sticks that smudge and dust. Marks run from whisper-gray to deep black, edges blur where a finger dragged through them, and the surface looks touched. Feels expressive, moody, and physical. |
| **Marker** | Drawn in broad strokes that show overlap where they cross. Color is bold but slightly streaky, and edges bleed a little. Feels quick, casual, and energetic. |
| **Paint** | Built from brushstrokes with visible texture and mixing. Color shifts inside every shape and edges vary from crisp to lost. Feels crafted, rich, and expressive. |
| **Airbrush** | Sprayed in soft gradients with no visible strokes. Tones melt into each other and highlights glow. Feels smooth, retro, and dreamlike. |
| **Spray Paint** | Sprayed through stencils or freehand with visible overspray. Edges cut crisp where the stencil held and drift soft where the mist traveled, with drips where the paint ran heavy. Feels urban, rebellious, and immediate. |
| **Cut Paper** | Assembled from physical pieces layered on top of each other. Every shape has a hard cut edge and a slight lift where it sits. Feels tactile, playful, and handmade. |
| **Engraving** | Built from thousands of fine parallel lines that swell and thin to make tone, the way banknotes and old book plates were made. Tone comes entirely from how dense the lines run. Feels precise, antique, and authoritative. |
| **Carved Print** | Cut from a block and stamped, so every mark is a gouge. Lines are bold and slightly ragged, fills are solid, and white space is what got carved away. Feels rough, graphic, and old-world. |

**Hand**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Machine-Exact** | No trace of a human hand anywhere. Every curve is perfect, every repeat identical, every line the same weight throughout. Feels precise, controlled, and engineered. |
| **Tight Hand** | Drawn by hand but carefully. Lines land where they aim with only faint wobble, and shapes close cleanly. Feels careful, warm, and considered. |
| **Loose Hand** | Drawn quickly and confidently with visible variation. Lines overshoot corners, weights shift, and shapes sit slightly off. Feels relaxed, charming, and spontaneous. |
| **Raw Hand** | Drawn fast with no correction. Marks scratch, wobble, and miss, and the energy of making is the point. Feels urgent, rebellious, and unfiltered. |

**Dimension**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Flat** | No depth at all. Shapes are pure silhouette and fill, nothing casts a shadow, and the surface admits it is a surface. Feels graphic, clean, and contemporary. |
| **Soft Depth** | Mostly flat with small depth cues. Slight shadows lift elements off the ground and layers stack with gentle separation. Feels friendly, light, and approachable. |
| **Full Volume** | Forms are modeled with light and shade until they read as solid objects sitting in space. Highlights, core shadows, and cast shadows all appear, and every surface turns toward or away from the light. Feels dimensional, real, and weighty. |

### Composition

**Arrangement**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Centered** | The subject sits dead middle with equal room on every side. The frame locks stable and the eye goes straight on. Feels formal, confident, and direct. |
| **Symmetrical** | The two halves mirror each other around an axis. Order dominates, and any small break in the mirror becomes the focal point. Feels balanced, classical, and deliberate. |
| **Grid** | Elements snap to rows and columns. Everything has an assigned place and the structure shows. Feels ordered, systematic, and rational. |
| **Radial** | Elements spread outward from a center point like spokes. The eye starts in the middle and travels out and around. Feels dynamic, complete, and emblem-like. |
| **Diagonal** | The main elements run corner to corner. The frame picks up movement and lean. Feels active, energetic, and on the move. |
| **Asymmetric** | Weight sits deliberately to one side with empty space answering it. The imbalance is the design. Feels modern, tense, and styled. |
| **Scattered** | Elements spread across the whole field with no single anchor. The eye wanders and the surface reads as texture. Feels playful, abundant, and casual. |
| **Framed** | A border or surrounding device holds the content inside it. Everything points inward. Feels contained, finished, and presented. |

**Focus**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Single Focus** | One element clearly owns the frame and everything else steps back. The eye goes one place first, every time. Feels clear, decisive, and easy to read. |
| **Lead with Support** | One element leads but supporting pieces share the stage. The eye lands, then travels a planned second and third step. Feels layered, guided, and composed. |
| **Even Spread** | No element outranks the rest. The eye moves across the whole surface and the field itself is the subject. Feels democratic, rhythmic, and all-over. |

**Negative Space**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Minimal** | The frame is nearly full. Elements run close to each other and to the edges, with little air anywhere. Feels loud, dense, and maximal. |
| **Breathing** | Enough room between elements for everything to read separately. The space does its job and stays unnoticed. Feels comfortable, balanced, and natural. |
| **Generous** | Empty space takes a clear share of the frame. Elements sit apart and the room around them reads as a choice. Feels calm, premium, and considered. |
| **Dominant** | Emptiness owns the frame and the subject sits small inside it. The space is the statement. Feels quiet, isolated, and expensive. |

### Color

**Palette Mode**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Black and White** | No color at all, only black, white, and the grays between. Everything depends on contrast and shape. Feels graphic, timeless, and stripped to essentials. |
| **Monochrome** | One hue explored through its full range of light, medium, and dark. Tonal variety without color variety. Feels cohesive, restrained, and sophisticated. |
| **Duotone** | Exactly two colors carry the whole image, one playing dark and one playing light. The pairing becomes the identity. Feels bold, printed, and unmistakable. |
| **Limited Palette** | Three or four colors do all the work, each holding a clear job. The discipline shows. Feels designed, intentional, and confident. |
| **Full Palette** | Color runs free with as many hues as the work wants. Richness comes from variety. Feels open, vivid, and abundant. |

**Saturation**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Desaturated** | Color pulled back toward gray. Hues are present but muted and quiet. Feels restrained, somber, and contemporary. |
| **Natural** | Color at a believable level, nothing pushed or pulled. Feels honest, neutral, and easy. |
| **Saturated** | Color pushed forward, vivid and rich. The palette asserts itself. Feels vibrant, bold, and energetic. |
| **Hyper-Saturated** | Color at its limit, almost artificial in its intensity. The color itself becomes the event. Feels extreme, graphic, and stylized. |

**Temperature**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Warm** | The palette leans golden, red, and amber. The whole graphic radiates toward the viewer. Feels comfortable, friendly, and inviting. |
| **Neutral** | No lean either way. Colors render as themselves without a cast. Feels balanced, honest, and professional. |
| **Cool** | The palette leans blue, green, and violet. The graphic sits back from the viewer. Feels modern, calm, and precise. |
| **Mixed** | Warm and cool share the frame, usually split between subject and ground. The contrast adds dimension. Feels layered, dynamic, and deliberate. |

**Harmony**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Monochromatic** | One hue in many values. Unity is total and tone does the talking. Feels cohesive, calm, and refined. |
| **Complementary** | Opposites on the wheel pushed together, orange against blue, red against green. Both get louder. Feels punchy, vibrant, and high-energy. |
| **Analogous** | Two to four neighbors on the wheel that share undertones. Colors flow into each other without friction. Feels harmonious, natural, and easy. |
| **Triadic** | Three colors evenly spaced around the wheel. High variety held in structural balance. Feels bold, playful, and lively. |
| **Split-Complementary** | A base color plus the two neighbors of its opposite. Contrast without the head-on clash. Feels dynamic, nuanced, and stable. |

**Hierarchy**

| Role | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Dominant (40-60%)** | The color holding the most area. Sets the temperature and mood of the whole graphic and is the first thing the viewer registers. |
| **Supporting (20-30%)** | Bridges the dominant and the accents. Creates flow and connects the parts of the composition. |
| **Accent (5-10%)** | The smallest and most noticed color. Pulls the eye to the point that matters and is usually the most saturated thing in the frame. |
| **Neutral Foundation (20-30%)** | Whites, grays, blacks, and earth tones with no strong hue. Gives the active colors room to work. |

### Treatment

**Print Process**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Clean Digital** | No process marks at all. Color is perfectly even, edges are exact, and the surface is invisible. Feels modern, polished, and weightless. |
| **Halftone** | The image is built from a visible dot pattern that grows and shrinks to make tone. Up close it is dots, at distance it is image. Feels printed, commercial, and retro. |
| **Screen Print** | Ink laid in thick, slightly imperfect layers, one color at a time. Colors sit a touch off-register and fills carry tiny gaps. Feels handmade, bold, and limited-run. |
| **Risograph** | Bright, slightly fluorescent ink in grainy layers with visible misregistration. Overlaps blend into surprise colors. Feels zine-like, casual, and current. |
| **Letterpress** | Ink pressed into the surface so every mark carries a physical bite. Edges are crisp with slight squeeze. Feels crafted, traditional, and premium. |
| **Photocopy** | High-contrast black with breakup in the fills and noise in the grays. Detail degrades and edges fry. Feels raw, punk, and urgent. |

**Grain**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **None** | Surfaces are perfectly smooth with no texture in the flats. Feels clean, digital, and commercial. |
| **Fine** | A whisper of texture in fills and shadows, present but not announcing itself. Feels organic and warm. |
| **Medium** | Visible grain across the whole surface that gives every area a tactile quality. Feels analog, textured, and deliberate. |
| **Heavy** | Grain dominates the surface and eats into edges and detail. Feels gritty, raw, and lo-fi. |

**Edges**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Crisp** | Every edge cuts clean with no softness anywhere. Shapes separate perfectly. Feels exact, sharp, and digital. |
| **Softened** | Edges ease slightly into their surroundings. The graphic loses its clinical bite. Feels gentle, friendly, and worn-in. |
| **Rough** | Edges break, chatter, and vary along their length. Every boundary carries texture. Feels handmade, energetic, and imperfect. |
| **Distressed** | Edges are eaten away, cracked, and broken. The graphic looks like it survived something. Feels aged, rugged, and storied. |

**Wear**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Fresh** | Straight off the press. Nothing faded, nothing scuffed, every surface at full strength. Feels new, confident, and current. |
| **Lightly Handled** | Small signs of life, slight fade, soft corners. Still clean but no longer untouched. Feels familiar and lived-with. |
| **Worn** | Clear fading, scuffs, and loss across the surface. Time shows everywhere. Feels nostalgic, honest, and loved. |
| **Aged** | Heavy fade, stains, and damage. More history than surface left. Feels antique, found, and archival. |

**Finish**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Matte** | No shine anywhere. Light dies on the surface and color reads soft and deep. Feels understated, premium, and calm. |
| **Satin** | A low sheen that lifts color slightly without reflecting the room. Feels balanced, finished, and quietly polished. |
| **Gloss** | Full shine with highlights riding the surface. Color pops hard. Feels loud, slick, and commercial. |
| **Foil** | Metallic material stamped onto the surface, gold, silver, or colored chrome. It catches real light, shifting between bright and dark as the viewing angle changes. Feels luxurious, celebratory, and special. |
| **Emboss** | The surface itself is raised or pressed where the design sits. The graphic gains physical relief and reads by shadow as much as by ink. Feels premium, tactile, and crafted. |
| **Spot Gloss** | Shine applied only to chosen areas while the rest stays matte. The contrast makes the glossed parts float above the surface. Feels considered, subtle, and high-end. |

### Mood

| Option | What It Feels Like |
|---|---|
| **Clean** | Flat color, generous space, exact edges, and nothing extra anywhere. The graphic looks effortless and engineered at once. Feels professional, clear, and trustworthy. |
| **Bold** | Heavy shapes, loud scale jumps, and maximum contrast. The graphic hits before it explains. Feels confident, loud, and unmissable. |
| **Playful** | Bouncy shapes, bright color, and loose rules. Nothing takes itself seriously and small jokes hide in the details. Feels joyful, light, and friendly. |
| **Elegant** | Thin weights, restrained palette, and generous emptiness. Every element earns its place. Feels refined, expensive, and composed. |
| **Raw** | Rough marks, broken edges, and no cleanup. The making shows and the speed shows. Feels urgent, honest, and unpolished. |
| **Technical** | Thin uniform lines, visible structure, and measured spacing, like a diagram that became design. Feels precise, intelligent, and engineered. |
| **Friendly** | Rounded everything, soft palette, simple forms. The graphic smiles. Feels warm, approachable, and safe. |
| **Retro** | Period palette, period letterforms, and period process marks working together. The graphic belongs to a decade on purpose. Feels nostalgic, charming, and knowing. |
| **Minimal** | The least possible. One or two elements, lots of nothing, and total restraint. Feels quiet, modern, and certain. |
| **Luxurious** | Deep color, rich surface, fine detail, and perfect finish. Everything reads as cost. Feels premium, indulgent, and exclusive. |
| **Energetic** | Diagonals, clashes, repetition, and saturated color. The surface vibrates. Feels alive, fast, and loud. |
| **Quiet** | Muted palette, gentle forms, even spacing. Nothing fights for attention. Feels calm, contemplative, and still. |
| **Serious** | Dark or neutral palette, restrained shapes, and zero decoration. The graphic states its business and stops. Feels weighty, formal, and adult. |
| **Rebellious** | Clashing color, broken rules, rough process marks, and deliberate ugliness where it stings. The graphic picks a fight with good taste. Feels defiant, loud, and unapologetic. |
| **Futuristic** | Chrome, gradients, glow, and geometry that looks machine-born. The graphic points forward and leaves the present behind. Feels sleek, advanced, and synthetic. |
| **Handcrafted** | Visible hand in every mark, imperfect repeats, and materials that show their making. Care reads in the flaws that were kept. Feels warm, sincere, and personal. |

## Lower Level

### Linework

**Construction**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Line-Built** | Drawn as outlines with little or no fill. The line does all the describing. Feels light, open, and sketched. |
| **Shape-Built** | Built from solid filled shapes with no outlines. Silhouette and color do the describing. Feels bold, solid, and graphic. |
| **Mixed** | Lines and fills share the work, outlines holding shapes or breaking free of them. Feels rich, illustrative, and layered. |

**Weight**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Hairline** | The thinnest line that still reads. Delicate structure with no visual weight. Feels fine, technical, and precious. |
| **Light** | Thin but present. The line describes without asserting. Feels elegant, calm, and exact. |
| **Regular** | Medium weight balancing presence and detail. The default that disappears. Feels natural, steady, and versatile. |
| **Bold** | Thick lines that carry real weight. The line becomes a shape of its own. Feels strong, friendly, and direct. |
| **Heavy** | Lines so thick they dominate the graphic. The gaps shrink and the work goes dense. Feels chunky, loud, and unmissable. |

**Stroke Behavior**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Uniform** | The line holds one thickness from start to end, the monoline look. Nothing about the stroke suggests a hand or a tool. Feels even, modern, and systematic. |
| **Swelling** | The line breathes thick and thin along its length, the way a brush or flexible nib responds to pressure. Every curve carries the rhythm of the stroke that made it. Feels calligraphic, lively, and warm. |
| **Tapered** | Strokes start or end in points, thick in the body and vanishing at the tips. Marks look like they were flicked onto the surface. Feels quick, sharp, and gestural. |

**Fills**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Open** | No fill at all, just the line and the surface. Feels airy, quick, and unfinished on purpose. |
| **Flat** | Solid, even color edge to edge. No light, no depth, pure shape. Feels clean, modern, and graphic. |
| **Shaded** | Fills carry gradients or modeled tone. Form starts to round. Feels soft, dimensional, and rendered. |
| **Hatched** | Tone built from repeated lines or marks. Texture and value arrive together. Feels traditional, crafted, and editorial. |
| **Textured** | Fills carry pattern, grain, or surface instead of smooth color. Every shape has a material. Feels tactile, rich, and worked. |

### Form

**Shape Language**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Geometric** | Built from circles, squares, and triangles, ruler-and-compass shapes. Order shows everywhere. Feels rational, stable, and engineered. |
| **Geometric Softened** | Geometric foundations with the corners and tension eased. Structure without severity. Feels modern, friendly, and balanced. |
| **Organic** | Drawn from nature's curves, irregular, flowing, and asymmetric. Nothing repeats exactly. Feels alive, soft, and human. |
| **Mixed** | Geometry and organic shapes working against each other deliberately. The contrast is the energy. Feels dynamic, curated, and tense. |

**Corners**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Sharp** | Corners come to exact points. The shape carries tension and precision. Feels technical, serious, and edgy. |
| **Eased** | A slight rounding that takes off the bite without announcing itself. Feels professional, calm, and refined. |
| **Rounded** | Clearly rounded corners that read at a glance. The shape relaxes. Feels friendly, soft, and approachable. |
| **Pill** | Rounding pushed to the maximum, every end a full curve. The shape goes bouncy. Feels playful, cute, and harmless. |

**Complexity**

| Option | What It Does and What It Produces |
|---|---|
| **Silhouette** | Boiled down to the outline alone. One shape carries the whole idea. Feels iconic, instant, and confident. |
| **Simplified** | Just enough interior detail to explain the subject. Everything else is gone. Feels clear, modern, and designed. |
| **Detailed** | Real interior drawing, texture, and secondary elements. The eye has places to go. Feels rich, illustrative, and generous. |
| **Intricate** | Maximum density of detail, the kind that rewards leaning in. The complexity is the spectacle. Feels ornate, crafted, and obsessive. |
