**Run on demand** — per expression, after the board pass has named it and its folder holds the raw material. This is the bridge from reference-plus-commentary to a documented expression. (The app's reference-doc feature does a version of this; this is the local, folder-based run of it.)

# Expression Doc Writer

Write one expression's `doc.md` — the shape in `03 expressions/_expression-template/doc.md` — from the raw material in its folder.

**Reads:** `03 expressions/[name]/feedback.md` (Buck's raw commentary — the primary source) · `refs/` including each reference's own markdown commentary where it carried one from round one · `explorations/` and any capture.md files in it · `blocks/controls - composition.md` or `controls - graphic.md` (which controls exist for this type) · `blocks/ad logic - composition.md` or `ad logic - graphics.md` (the option vocabulary)
**Writes:** `03 expressions/[name]/doc.md`. Buck QAs every doc before it's treated as real.

## How

1. **Type it.** Composition or graphic, and the subtype (web section, texture, lettering, etc.) — the controls doc for that type is your control list.
2. **Distill "what it is" and "what Buck likes"** from the feedback, in his terms. Keep the tensions he names ("they clash, but that's good tension") — those carry more direction than the likes.
3. **Lock only what the commentary covers.** Go control by control through the type's control list. Where Buck's commentary (or the reference's own markdown) actually speaks to a control, write it up as keywords, terms, and phrases — off the reference visual plus his words, in the AD-logic vocabulary where it fits. Expect 4–6 locked out of the full set, not all of them.
4. **Everything else is leave-open.** Do not fill uncovered controls from taste, from the reference's look, or from what would make the doc feel complete — an uncovered control is variation space, and cycling it is where variations come from. Name specific directions worth cycling only if Buck's commentary hinted at them.
5. **Negatives and open questions.** What he ruled out, what died nearby, and what the expression is waiting on (logo script, the texture) — flagged, not decided.

## The bar

A total stranger — a great creative or an AI — could pick up this folder and continue the expression without this conversation. If the feedback is too thin to lock a control honestly, say so in the doc and leave it open rather than padding. Flag ambiguity to Buck instead of resolving it.
